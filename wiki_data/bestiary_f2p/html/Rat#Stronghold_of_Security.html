Rats are small animals commonly found throughout Gielinor, mostly in cities, sewers, and dungeons. They respawn rapidly, and are not marked on the minimap.

Although most rats have no drops, the rats in the Stronghold of Security have a 100% chance to drop bones.

Considered by most players to be the weakest monster in the game, rats are frequently used to train Magic by splashing.

Cats[edit | edit source]

Due to an update, the information in this article may be out-of-date.
Reason: Catchrates have been adjusted as part of a quality-of-life update for Ratcatchers.
You can help improve it by updating the information.

Rats can be chased by cats. When telling a cat to chase vermin, it will pounce at a rat in range, attempting to catch it. If your cat successfully catches at least 100 rats, you can talk to Gertrude to get a cat training medal, which has no special uses other than being a collectible. Furthermore, if you have a rat pole, up to six caught rats can be added to it as decoration.

Each type of cat has a different catch rate:

  • Kitten - 10%
  • Normal - 50%
  • Overgrown - 0% (cannot chase rats)
  • Wily - 70%
  • Lazy - 41%

Hellcats share the same catch rates as regular cats, depending on their type.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The rat was among one of the first monsters added to the game, as it was released when the RuneScape beta first launched alongside creatures such as goblins, spiders, ghosts, and its larger counterpart, the giant rat.
  • The regular and Stronghold of Security rat variants had festive overrides wearing red partyhats during the 2017, 2018, and 2019 Christmas events.
  • In RuneScape: The Official Handbook from 2006, it is stated that regular rats may weigh up to 70 pounds. This is likely an error in unit translation, as other weight and height statistics in the handbook are unrealistic - such as Guard Bandits weighing "up to 70lbs" and Giant Spiders having their height listed as 30 feet.
