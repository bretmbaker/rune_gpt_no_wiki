Wine of Zamorak is a secondary ingredient used in Herblore. It can be found on both floors of Asgarnia's Chaos Temple and the Chaos Temple south-west of the Chaos Fanatic in level 38 Wilderness, in which both locations have their wine respawn every 30 seconds. It can also be found at the end of the Deep Wilderness Dungeon, where it respawns every 6.6 seconds and is obtained in noted form if players have completed the hard Wilderness Diary. Respawn rates for wines are affected by the amount of players on a world, reducing the time to spawn with more players on a world; Asgarnia's Chaos Temple on high population worlds has an approximate respawn rate of 16 seconds, and Deep Wilderness Dungeon has an approximate respawn rate of every 4.2 seconds.

The wine can also be made by using Zamorak's grapes with a jug of water, requiring Level 65 Cooking and granting 200 Cooking experience.

Wines of Zamorak cannot be consumed, but are used as a secondary ingredient in creating ranging and bastion potions. The bloody grimoire states that the wine is incredibly unholy but has a sweet taste that makes it easily addictive.

Success chance[edit | edit source]

The chance for a jug of Zamorak's unfermented wine to successfully turn into a wine of Zamorak is about 50% at level 65, increasing to a 100% chance at level 98.[1] Note that mixing wines of Zamorak in large quantities is likely processed in the same way as regular jugs of wine are, meaning rolls are not made for each wine individually. Rather, the total amount of Zamorak's unfermented wines is likely divided into 15 batches, with a skilling success roll being performed for each of them, in addition to any of the unfermented wines left over from the division.

Obtaining[edit | edit source]

There are two ways to take a wine of Zamorak:

  • Casting Telekinetic Grab (Requiring 1 air rune, 1 law rune and 33 Magic). The player will be dealt damage and Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged and Magic stats slightly drained. On the ground floor[UK]1st floor[US] the monks will become aggressive to the caster.
  • At Asgarnia's Chaos Temple, you may physically take it while all of the monks are dead; however the damage and stat-draining effects will still occur. The monks respawn after 150 seconds.

Telekinetic Grab is by far the more popular method for obtaining the wine of Zamorak, as it is possible at both locations, the monks don't have to all be killed, and the wine is worth more than the cost of runes for Telekinetic Grab.

Considerations[edit | edit source]

It's recommended to bring wizard's mind bomb (F2P) or restore potions (Members) to restore your stats because if your Magic level is drained below 33 you won't be able to cast Telekinetic Grab.

Players who have completed the hard Wilderness Diary will receive wines of Zamorak in noted form from the Wilderness Chaos Temple. The temple is in a multi-combat area and is a frequent player killing spot due to the altar in the temple.

Since the wine takes about 20 seconds to spawn, other tasks such as using High Alchemy on a noted item or finishing unfinished bolts can be done at the same time, possibly making this method rich in money as well as experience.

A looting bag can be useful for collecting wine in both locations. Players obtaining wine in the non-Wilderness Temple can walk a short distance to the Wilderness north and enter level 1 Wilderness to deposit the wine in the bag, then proceed back to the Temple and gather more. If using this method, do not bring items you are not willing to lose.

Travel[edit | edit source]

Players who gather the wine for herblore or profit can access a bank by:

  • Taking the shortcut to Burthorpe located behind the temple (Requires completion of easy Falador Diary), and running to the bank in the Rogues' Den. (Recommended)
  • Walking to the Falador West Bank
  • Teleporting to any bank through such methods as ring of dueling or amulet of glory
  • Teleporting to Falador by sacrificing a slot for water runes (unless you have a Tome of Water equipped)
    • No teleport methods will work above level 30 Wilderness

Players can get back to the temple by:

  • Walking back
  • Using a Goblin Village Sphere (from Oldak, requiring Another Slice of H.A.M.) to teleport to Goblin Village and walking from there.
  • Using the Mind Altar Teleport from the Arceuus Spellbook to teleport to the Mind Altar and walking from there.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • "Zamorak" is not capitalised in the item name despite it being a god's name. This is because all items in Old School RuneScape use ucfirst capitalisation, wherein the first letter of a string is capitalised, but the rest is not.
  • On release, a wine of Zamorak did not spawn in the Wilderness temple. This was added with an update on 2 March 2017.
