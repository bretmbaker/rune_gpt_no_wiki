Thammaron's sceptre (a) is the autocasting variant of Thammaron's sceptre, requiring level 60 in Magic to wield.

By selecting the "Swap" option on the sceptre, players can switch between the regular sceptre (a powered staff) and the autocasting sceptre, which can autocast offensive combat spells from the standard spellbook (including Flames of Zamorak) and the Ancient Magicks.

Passive effect[edit | edit source]

When Thammaron's sceptre (a) is charged with revenant ether, an additional 50% magic accuracy and damage boost is applied when attacking any NPC in the Wilderness, consuming 1 ether per attack whether the player hits or not. It is applied to any combat spells cast when it is equipped.

This effect will stack with a Slayer helmet (i) or a Salve amulet(i) and their variants, but not both simultaneously. This makes the sceptre the second best magic weapon in the Wilderness after the accursed sceptre, surpassing the damage output of a trident of the seas or swamp with the additional capability of attacking any hostile players, something which both tridents are incapable of doing.

Charging and death[edit | edit source]

To use the sceptre's passive effect, it must first be activated with 1,000 revenant ether (costing 175,000 coins). The 1,000 ether used to activate the sceptre does not count towards the usage of the passive effect, thus additional ether must be added (up to 16,000) to increase the sceptre's damage output. Players can configure the charges while the sceptre is in their bank by right-clicking on Configure-Charges, as long as players have the required materials. The hourly cost of constantly using this weapon to cast spells is 1,200 ether or 210,000 coins, as spells can only be cast every 5 game ticks.

The ether can be safely removed by selecting the right-click Uncharge option on the sceptre, after which the sceptre becomes tradeable again.

Upon death within the Wilderness, any revenant ether stored within the sceptre will always be dropped, even if it is one of the player's protected items. Because of this, despite the sceptre being able to store a maximum of 17,000 revenant ether, it is recommended to store the minimum amount required for a trip into the Wilderness (in addition to the 1,000 ether required to activate the weapon).
