Moonrise Wines is a book which offers details and tasting notes for various wines produced in Varlamore, many available at the winery shop. It can be obtained by searching the eastern bookshelf on the upper level of the Moonrise Brewery and Winery.

After it has been obtained once, it can be re-obtained from the bookcase in a player-owned house.

The following wines are catalogued in this book:

  • Blackbird Red
  • Chilhuac Red
  • Eclipse Red
  • Principum Red
  • Ixcoztic White
  • Metztonalli White
  • Tonameyo White
  • Fortis Ash White
  • Chichilihui Rosé
  • Imperial Rosé
  • Xochipaltic Rosé

Effects[edit | edit source]

All wines heal 16 Hitpoints and drain Attack by 5.

  • Blackbird Red boosts Hunter by 1, while draining Slayer by 1.
  • Chilhuac Red boosts Firemaking by 1, while draining Cooking by 1.
  • Eclipse Red boosts Woodcutting by 1, while draining Fletching by 1.
  • Ixcoztic White boosts Farming by 1, while draining Herblore by 1.
  • Metztonalli White boosts Runecraft by 1, while draining Firemaking by 1.
  • Tonameyo White restores 1 prayer point, while draining Thieving by 1.
  • Chichilihui Rosé boosts Herblore by 1, while draining Farming by 1.
  • Imperial Rosé boosts Slayer by 1, while draining Agility by 1.
  • Principum Red, Fortis Ash White, and Xochipaltic Rosé are unobtainable.

Transcript[edit | edit source]

The following text is transcluded from Transcript:Moonrise wines (book).
Introduction

The Moonrise Brewery and Winery is the oldest producer of alcohol in Varlamore, with them being best known for their unmatchable variety of wines.

This guide will help you decode wine terms and tasting notes, enhancing your appreciation and enjoyment of the most famous Moonrise Wines.

Key Terms

Flavours:
The distinct taste characteristics found in a wine.

Body:
The weight or fullness of a wine on the palate.

Tannin:
The textural component that gives a wine its structure and bitterness.

Acidity:
The level of tartness or crispness in a wine, contributing to its freshness.

Sweetness:
The degree of sweetness present in a wine, ranging from dry to sweet.

Food Pairings:
Recommended foods that complement a wine's flavors and structure.

Blackbird Red

A smooth and rich Aldarin Red that's said to flutter on the tongue.

Flavours: Redberry, dwellberry, chocolate and cinnamon.

Body: 4/5
Tannin: 3/5
Acidity: 2/5
Sweetness: 3/5

Food Pairings: Grilled bird and earthy mushroom dishes.

Chilhuac Red

A smoky and spicy Tlati Red that's known for its dark colour.

Flavours: Cherry, jangerberry, marrentill and mixed spices.

Body: 5/5
Tannin: 4/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 1/5

Food Pairings: Spicy curries, roasted meats and kebabs.

Eclipse Red

A full-bodied Aldarin Red that's generally considered the finest wine available.

Flavours: Redberry, dwellberry, oak and teak.

Body: 5/5
Tannin: 5/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 2/5

Food Pairings: Roasted meats and meaty pies.

Principum Red

An incredibly rare Aldarin Red that was infamously used to poison the Kourend royal family.

Flavours: Redberry, dragonfruit, dried cocoa and marrentill.

Body: 5/5
Tannin: 4/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 2/5

Food Pairings: Grilled meats and rich savory dishes.

Ixcoztic White

A crisp and citrusy Aldarin White that's popular for all occasions.

Flavours: Apple, dwellberry, lemon and lime.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 1/5
Acidity: 4/5
Sweetness: 1/5

Food Pairings: Shellfish and light salads.

Metztonalli White

A light and refreshing Aldarin White that's favoured on warm summer days.

Flavours: Papaya, lemon, lime and marrentill.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 1/5
Acidity: 5/5
Sweetness: 1/5

Food Pairings: Fresh seafood, tangy curries and light vegetable dishes.

Tonameyo White

A rich and bold Darkfrost White that's considered by some to be sacred.

Flavours: Apple, jangerberry, oak and vanilla.

Body: 4/5
Tannin: 2/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 2/5

Food Pairings: Roasted poultry and lobster.

Fortis Ash White

A highly aromatic Fortis White that's no longer produced due to vine rot.

Flavours: Apple, lime, white lily and oil.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 1/5
Acidity: 5/5
Sweetness: 3/5

Food Pairings: Spicy curries and fruity desserts.

Chichilihui Rosé

A light and dry Tlati Rosé that's known for its floral elegance.

Flavours: Strawberry, dragonfruit, rose petal and rosemary.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 2/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 2/5

Food Pairings: Grilled salmon, light soups and salads.

Imperial Rosé

A complex and herby Tempestus Rosé that's famously served at the College of Bards.

Flavours: Watermelon, pineapple, marrentill and tarromin.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 2/5
Acidity: 4/5
Sweetness: 1/5

Food Pairings: Light appetisers, seafood and summer salads.

Xochipaltic Rosé

A sweet and pale Avium Rosé that's no longer produced due to vine rot.

Flavours: Peach, strawberry, white lily and orange blossom.

Body: 3/5
Tannin: 1/5
Acidity: 3/5
Sweetness: 4/5

Food Pairings: Light desserts, fruit pies and soft cheeses.
