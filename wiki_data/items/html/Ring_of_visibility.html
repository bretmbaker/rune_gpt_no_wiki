The ring of visibility is a special ring that can be obtained from Rasolo the merchant, after the player retrieves his gilded cross for him during the quest Desert Treasure I. Wearing the ring lets the player see mysterious ghosts and other objects that are normally invisible. The ring of visibility cannot be made using the Crafting skill. It cannot be equipped in the Lassar Undercity.

If the player dies with the ring equipped or in their inventory, they will always lose it even if they had less than 3 items (or four with Protect Item). The two exceptions are if the player dies while fighting Damis during Desert Treasure I, in which case they will keep the ring like normal, or if the player dies in the Shadow Realm during Desert Treasure II, in which case it will be kept or appear in your gravestone loot. If lost, the ring can be reclaimed from Rasolo, by talking to him, west of the Fishing Guild and south of the Baxtorian Falls. It may be possible for the ring to not remain in the player's inventory upon death, in this event the player can only retrieve their items from Death's Office as they will not be able to use the ghostly ladder without the ring.

After completion of Desert Treasure II, the ring of shadows can be used as a replacement to the ring of visibility.

Uses[edit | edit source]

  • Completing parts of Desert Treasure II
  • Accessing the Shadow Dungeon during and after Desert Treasure I
  • Seeing the mysterious ghosts for the Curse of the Empty Lord miniquest
  • Seeing General Khazard and his scouts as part of The General's Shadow
  • Seeing the Shaeded Beast in the Forthos Dungeon
  • Seeing the scout in the Ghorrock Dungeon
  • Seeing the ghost on top of Mynydd
  • Seeing the Ancient Ghosts on the Isle of Souls
  • Seeing the ghosts in the Ruins of Camdozaal
  • In some Treasure Trails
  • Seeing the knights in the the lobby of the Hallowed Sepulchre after encountering them while looting chests
  • Starting Hopespear's Will after completion of Land of the Goblins
  • Making money via High alching rings of visibility

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The ring's appearance as a plain, golden ring may be a reference to the One Ring from J.R.R. Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit. The One Ring shares this appearance, as well as the ability to see things others cannot.
