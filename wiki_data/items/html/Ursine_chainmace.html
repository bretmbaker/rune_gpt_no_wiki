The ursine chainmace is an upgraded variant of Viggora's chainmace, requiring level 70 in Attack to wield. It is created by combining Viggora's chainmace with the claws of Callisto. This process requires level 85 Smithing; players without the required level to combine the items may ask Andros Mai in Ferox Enclave to do so instead, for a fee of 500,000 coins. This item counts as a Zamorakian item in the God Wars Dungeon.

The chainmace is identical in function to Viggora's chainmace, but with increased stats and a special attack.

Passive effect[edit | edit source]

When an ursine chainmace is charged with revenant ether, an additional 50% melee accuracy and damage boost is applied when attacking any NPC in the Wilderness, consuming 1 ether per attack whether the player hits or not. Attacking with the mace outside of the Wilderness will still consume charges.

This effect will stack with a Slayer helmet or a Salve amulet and their variants, but not both simultaneously. This makes it the best melee weapon in the Wilderness for PvM, surpassing the maximum hit of an elder maul, while having the same attack speed as an abyssal whip.

Special attack[edit | edit source]

The ursine chainmace has a special attack, Bear Down, which consumes 50% of the player's special attack energy and is only usable whilst charged. The special attack hits an enemy with doubled accuracy, and upon a successful hit, also deals 20 damage over 6 seconds, prevents running for 3.6 seconds (6 ticks), and reduces the target's Agility level by 20.

The accuracy roll for Bear Down is based on the offensive bonuses of one's currently select style, which is rolled against an opponent's slash defences.[1]

Charging and death[edit | edit source]

To use the chainmace's passive effect, it must first be activated with 1,000 revenant ether (costing 175,000 coins). The 1,000 ether used to activate the chainmace does not count towards the usage of the passive effect, thus additional ether must be added (up to 16,000) to increase the chainmace's damage output. Players can configure the charges while the chainmace is in their bank by right-clicking on Configure-Charges, as long as players have the required materials. The hourly cost of constantly using this weapon is 1,500 ether or 262,500 coins.

The ether can be safely removed by selecting the right-click Uncharge option on the chainmace, after which the chainmace becomes tradeable again.

Upon death within the Wilderness, any revenant ether stored within the chainmace will always be dropped, even if it is one of the player's protected items. Because of this, despite the chainmace being able to store a maximum of 17,000 revenant ether, it is recommended to store the minimum amount required for a trip into the Wilderness (in addition to the 1,000 ether required to activate the weapon).

(update)

The Ursine chainmace supports a bank item option for changing charges.

3 May 2023
(update)
  • The item now prompts players to input the amount of ether they would like to add if there is another Wilderness weapon in their inventory.
  • The ‘Check’ inventory option has been replaced with ’Wield’ when the Bank is open.

(update)

Players can now check the number of charges in the Ursine chainmace while in the Bank interface, via a new ‘Check’ option.

(update)
  • An issue has been fixed in which players were able to avoid the slow effect of the special attack.
  • An issue has been fixed where the special attack did not damage Callisto, Venenatis, or Vet'ion.
