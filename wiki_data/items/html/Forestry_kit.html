The Forestry kit is an item worn in the cape slot used to store a variety of Forestry-related items, such as anima-infused bark and tree leaves. It can be obtained for free from the Forestry Shop in Draynor Village, Prifddinas or Seers' Village. The Forestry kit is required for players to spawn events, meaning at least one player chopping the tree needs to either be wearing one or have one in their inventory.

Players can quickly empty the contents of the kit by right-clicking "Use" on the item whilst the bank interface is open. This empties the contents into the inventory, which can then be deposited into the bank.

The contents of the Forestry kit are accessible from any Forestry kit in the player's possession, but if the player destroys their last kit, all contents will be lost.

The log basket, Forestry kit, and Forestry basket can all be stored in the magic wardrobe within the costume room of a player-owned house.

Functions[edit | edit source]

Usage as a run energy source[edit | edit source]

When filled with Forester's rations, the Forestry kit can work as a wearable source of run energy if the player is using an axe. When chopping trees successfully, rations have a 30% chance to restore 20% run energy if the player's energy is under 90%. Players who have a Forestry kit with rations whilst having an axe equipped (or in their inventory) can restore their run energy whilst navigating through Gielinor. The Woodcutting to run energy "conversion" is particularly useful whilst performing tasks that integrate Woodcutting as part of the activity such as tree runs and hunter rumours.

Forester's rations themselves have low requirements to make with level 35 Woodcutting and Cooking, cooked meat/cooked fish, and leaves, which are passively gained while Woodcutting. Low levelled accounts, skill pures, or accounts without means to purchase (or create) stamina or energy potions may find this method useful for recovering energy.

Usage as a Woodcutting boost[edit | edit source]

Players with the kit can receive the benefits of either the lumberjack or Forestry outfit without having to wear it by storing it in the clothes pouch, then storing it in the Forestry kit. Doing so requires level 50 Crafting and 50 Woodcutting, and also requires purchasing the clothes pouch blueprint. Players can also store the cape pouch in addition to the clothes pouch with level 99 Woodcutting. The additional experience from the outfit (and cape if stored using the cape pouch) will be awarded even if the Forestry kit remains in the inventory. Wearing any other piece of equipment possessing a set effect of its own will disable the effect of the corresponding stored lumberjack or Forestry outfit piece.

Usage as a Forestry basket[edit | edit source]

Players can combine and detach the Forestry kit with a log basket and sturdy harness to create a temporary Forestry basket, adding the functionality of the basket to the equippable kit. However, most players opt not to make the Forestry basket because it must be in the player's inventory to bank the logs. More specifically, players need to go through the Forestry basket dialogue to view the Forestry kit, check the log basket, and also open/close the log basket.
