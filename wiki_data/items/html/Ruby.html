Rubies are precious gems used mainly in Crafting. They can be cut from uncut rubies with a chisel at level 34 Crafting, yielding 85 experience.

A one-time reward of 2 cut rubies can be obtained by using a chisel and hammer on Thammaron's throne during The Golem quest. Completing Olaf's Quest rewards 4 cut rubies.

In exchange for 250 cut rubies, 500 fire runes (combination runes will not work), 100 cosmic runes, and 1 Digsite pendant of any charge Curator Haig Halen will award the player with a Curator's medallion. The medallion is needed to build a Mounted digsite pendant in the portal nexus of a player-owned house.

Other[edit | edit source]

Cut rubies can also be used to make the following items:

  • 2 cut rubies are used during the Family Crest quest along with two 'perfect' gold bars to create the 'perfect' ring and 'perfect' necklace.
  • 250 cut rubies, 500 fire runes, 100 cosmic runes, and 1 digsite pendant can be exchanged for a curator's medallion, required for mounting a Digsite pendant in a player-owned house.
  • Ruby sickle (b), an enhanced silver sickle (b) required during Sins of the Father.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
Date unknown

Gems available on free-to-play worlds can no longer be crushed with a hammer to receive a members object (crushed gem).

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Ruby was used as one of the tiers of post count badges on the official Runescape Forums. Players needed to have made 100,000 posts to be given the ruby badge.
