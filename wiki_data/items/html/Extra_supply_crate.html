Extra supply crates are obtained by exchanging certain items to Ignisia. Unlike regular pulls from the reward cart, these typically only come with one roll on the drop table.

The items players can exchange are:

  • Pyromancer outfit
  • Warm gloves
  • Bruma torch
  • Tome of fire (empty)

Note that players cannot receive these items from the exchanged crates, nor can they receive burnt pages or the Phoenix pet. Receiving a dragon axe is possible.

Handing in a full set of the pyromancer outfit (hood, garb, legs, and boots) will give players an extra supply crate with five rolls on the drop table rather than the regular one. This is a bonus of one when compared to handing in the components individually.[1]

Rewards[edit | edit source]

If a material drop is selected (herbs, ores, fish, gems, seeds, and logs), the chances of obtaining a higher quality drop depends upon players' level in its associated skill; Herblore, Mining, Fishing, Crafting, Farming, and Woodcutting respectively.[2] At the highest point, low levelled materials are removed from the drop table. While players with low levels in those skills can get rare materials, the drop rate is very low, but will increase with that skill level.

Logs[edit | edit source]

There is about a 1/9 chance to drop on the logs subtable. The exact drop rates on this subtable are based on the player's Woodcutting level; a higher level player is more likely to receive magic logs than a lower level player. All of the logs can be received with a much lower woodcutting level than it would take to cut them from trees; most notably this allows Ironmen to get Magic logs up to 25 levels earlier.
