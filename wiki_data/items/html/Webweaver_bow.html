The webweaver bow is an upgraded variant of Craw's bow, requiring level 70 in Ranged to wield. It is created by combining Craw's bow with the Fangs of Venenatis. This process requires level 85 Fletching. Players without the required level to combine the items may ask Derse Venator in Ferox Enclave to do so instead, for a fee of 500,000. The item counts as an Armadylean item in the God Wars Dungeon.

The bow is identical in function to Craw's bow, but with increased stats and a special attack.

Passive effect[edit | edit source]

When attacking any NPC in the Wilderness, an additional 50% ranged accuracy and damage boost is applied. This effect will stack multiplicatively with a Slayer helmet (i) or a Salve amulet(i) and their variants, but not both simultaneously. This makes it the strongest ranged weapon in the Wilderness, surpassing the damage output of a toxic blowpipe with dragon darts.

Special attack[edit | edit source]

The webweaver bow has a special attack, Swarm, which consumes 50% of the player's special attack energy. The special attack hits an enemy four times in succession with doubled accuracy, each dealing up to 40%, rounded up, of the player's max hit.[1]

Each hit also has a chance to inflict poison, starting at four damage.

Charging and death[edit | edit source]

To use the bow, it must first be activated with 1,000 revenant ether (costing 175,000 coins). The 1,000 ether used to activate the bow does not count towards the ammo usage of the bow, thus additional ether must be added (up to 16,000) to fire it. Players can configure the charges while the weapon is in their bank by right-clicking on Configure-Charges, as long as players have the required materials. Using the bow requires revenant ether whether inside the Wilderness or not, but the 50% ranged accuracy and damage boost only applies while inside the Wilderness.

The bow uses 1 revenant ether per shot and fires 2000 shots per hour on the rapid attack style. Thus the hourly cost of constantly using this weapon is 2,000 ether or 350,000 coins on Rapid, or 1,500 ether or 262,500 coins on Accurate/Longrange.

The ether can be safely removed by selecting the right-click Uncharge option on the bow, after which the bow becomes tradeable again.

Upon death within the Wilderness, any revenant ether stored as ammo within the bow will always be dropped and the initial 1,000 activation ether will always be provided to the killer, even if it is one of the player's protected items. Therefore, dying with an activated bow incurs a minimum loss of 1,000 ether. Despite the bow being able to store a maximum of 17,000 revenant ether, it is recommended to store the minimum amount required for a trip into the Wilderness (in addition to the 1,000 ether required to activate the weapon).

If the death is caused by an NPC the ether can be recovered in the death office.
