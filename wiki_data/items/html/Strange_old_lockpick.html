The strange old lockpick is a potential reward from the Hallowed Sepulchre. It allows any door within Barrows to be opened when used on the doors. It comes with 50 charges, using one after it is used to open a set of double-doors. The lockpick is tradeable until it has been used. After all 50 charges have been used it disappears. When running low on charges, the High Level Alchemy spell can be used to recuperate some of the costs back, since its alch value stays high even when used.

The strange old lockpick provides a better success rate for opening coffins within the Hallowed Sepulchre compared to a normal lockpick.[1] Additionally this lockpick will speed up looting coffins compared to having a normal lockpick or no lockpick. It does not use any charges when used in the Hallowed Sepulchre.

Despite being an improved variant of a lockpick within the Hallowed Sepulchre, it cannot be used in place of a regular lockpick outside of the Hallowed Sepulchre.[2]

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
5 February 2025
(update)

An issue has been fixed where the strange old lockpick degraded erroneously in certain Barrows corridors.

9 July 2020
(update)

Fully charged strange old lockpicks now properly speed up looting coffins in the Hallowed Sepulchre; previously, only partially charged lockpicks provided the effect due to an oversight.

25 June 2020
(update)

Strange old lockpicks will now speed up the looting of coffins within the Hallowed Sepulchre in comparison to having regular lockpicks/no lockpicks; charges will not be consumed when using them there.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Its name is an allusion to the strange old man, the mysterious gravekeeper of the Barrows.
Bug:
If the strange old lockpick is in a looting bag and the player dies, the lockpick will remain in the looting bag until it is obtained again, where it will be stored. The only way to retrieve it as an ultimate ironman is to obtain a new looting bag and destroy it in the wilderness, dropping the strange old lockpick to the ground.
