A trouver parchment is a consumable scroll, purchased from Justine's stuff for the Last Shopper Standing for 18 Last Man Standing points or obtained as a drop from killing monsters in the Wilderness Slayer Cave.

The parchment is used to "lock" and keep untradeable items if killed in PvP combat. Players will need to give the parchment and pay 500,000 to Perdu for each item they lock; multiple items can be locked simultaneously, but will require additional parchments for each one.

At current prices, it costs 1,736,384 to purchase a parchment from the Grand Exchange and pay Perdu to use it on an item.

Use[edit | edit source]

Activating[edit | edit source]

Giving certain untradeable items and a trouver parchment to Perdu will lock the item to the player, giving the item an additional (l) suffix in its name (for example, "Fire cape (l)"). Once an item is locked, the parchment will ensure the locked item is kept in its unbroken state upon dying a player versus player death, even if that item would otherwise be destroyed (for example, when experiencing a player versus player death above level 20 Wilderness).

If the player experiences a player versus monster death, the locked status will be retained and the locked item will be kept in its unbroken state.

Unlike other items, Perdu will only charge 50,000 coins to lock bronze to adamant defenders.

Dying with a locked item[edit | edit source]

If a player is killed in a player versus player death and a locked item is not protected, the locked item will lose its locked status. This applies if the player experiences any PvP death, regardless of if it occurs at or below level 20 Wilderness or in a PvP World. When the locked status is removed, the parchment is deleted. If a player is killed in a player versus player death and a locked item is protected, the locked item will not lose its locked status. It should be noted that the Trouver parchment does not protect runes stored within a Rune pouch upon a player versus player death.

The killer will receive the locking fee (that is, the amount paid to Perdu), but not the item or the Trouver parchment itself.

If a locked item is removed from the game (such as from a player dropping an item in an instance and leaving), the item will still retain its locked status when it is re-obtained.

Removing a lock[edit | edit source]

Players also can remove the lock status on an item by using it on Perdu, in which he will return the Trouver parchment as well as 95% of the locking fee.

Upgrading a locked item[edit | edit source]

Some lockable items are also upgradeable, such as the rune pouch to the divine rune pouch. If an upgradeable item is locked, the lock must be removed before the item can be upgraded.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
3 May 2023
(update | poll)

The Wilderness Slayer Cave loot table now gives two Trouver Parchments when received rather than one.

18 May 2022
(update)
  • Trouver Parchment can now be used on the Leagues III version of Void Knight Equipment, Dragon Defenders (t) and Rune Defenders (t).
    • If the item is locked (l), upon PvP death the PKer will receive the value of the Trouver Parchment and the Ornament Kit and the regular version of the item will be returned to the owner. This occurs at all levels of the Wilderness.
    • If the item is not locked, upon PvP death the PKer will receive gold equivalent to the value of the item (minus the value of the Ornament Kit) and the Ornament Kit. At Wilderness levels below 20, the owner will receive a broken version of their original item. At Wilderness levels above 20, the owner will receive nothing.
9 March 2022
(update)

The amount of Last Man Standing points required to purchase a Trouver parchment has been further reduced from 30 to 18.

24 June 2020
(update)

The amount of coins dropped on PvP death is now 100% instead of 75%.

23 January 2020
(update)

The amount of Last Man Standing points required to purchase a Trouver parchment has been reduced from 50 to 30.
