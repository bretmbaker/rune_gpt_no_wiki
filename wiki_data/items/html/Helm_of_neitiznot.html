The helm of Neitiznot is a helmet awarded to players upon completing The Fremennik Isles quest and requires a Defence level of 55 to wear. While it is tradeable, players may only equip the helm if they have completed The Fremennik Isles quest.

Its stats are roughly akin to those of a berserker helm. However, the helm of Neitiznot has an additional +3 prayer bonus, +1 crush and +3 magic defence bonuses, and does not possess negative magic and ranged attack bonuses. The helmet's substantial bonuses, along with it being non-degradable and cheap, make it one of the most frequently used helmets.

Additional helms can be obtained from Mawnis Burowgar for 50,000 coins. Alternatively, for those who do not wish to travel to Neitiznot, Perdu will return the helm to players for 95,000 coins.

The helmet can be upgraded to the Neitiznot faceguard after completion of The Fremennik Exiles quest by combining the helm of Neitiznot and a basilisk jaw. You can only do this after asking Olaf the Bard for stories about basilisk jaws while having it in your inventory.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Upon release, casting High Level Alchemy on the helmet would yield more coins than it costs to buy; however, this was swiftly noticed and corrected by the developers.
  • The helm of Neitiznot was originally an untradeable item for the first day after release. However, this was quickly changed after player feedback due to it negatively affecting PvP and allowing people to risk less.
  • After the quest is complete, Mawnis Burowgar is seen without the helmet on as the player is named champion and the rightful owner of the helmet.
  • Despite being a tradeable item which can be obtained multiple times, there is implied to be only one helmet in existence; when the player loses it, Mawnis Burowgar's scouts find it.
