The venator bow is a two-handed ranged weapon that was once used by the venators, who were hunters and scouts that served the Zarosian Empire. Requiring level 80 in Ranged to wield, it is made by combining five venator shards. It can fire any type of arrow, including dragon arrows, and has an attack range of 6 (8 when using the "Longrange" combat style).

The bow can be charged with ancient essence to gain a unique passive effect that consumes charges, although it can also be used uncharged as a regular bow without degrading.

Passive effect and charging[edit | edit source]

The venator bow has a passive effect that requires ancient essence to use. When attacking a target in a multicombat area, the arrow will try to bounce to another target near the first. Then, the attack can bounce again, either back to the first target or to a third one. Each hit rolls accuracy independently, and the second and third hits both have a max hit equal to two-thirds of the original max hit, rounded down.

Bouncing arrows will only hit targets in a range of two squares from the target's centre tile (north-east tile for even-tiled NPCs). Bounces can still occur if an attack misses or one of the targets dies. A different sound effect plays depending on if the arrow hits one, two, or three times.

Each ancient essence adds 1 charge to the bow, with it being able to hold up to 50,000 charges (costing 9 coins per attack, and a total of 450,000 to fully charge it with 50,000 essence). Players can configure the charges while the weapon is in their bank by right-clicking on Configure-Charges, as long as players have the required materials. One charge is deducted per attack, and it will always lose one charge regardless of how many bounces occur from that projectile. Assuming the bow is used on rapid, the hourly rate of using it is 13,500 coins. The fully charged bow will last for 33.33 hours of continuous combat before reverting to its uncharged form.

The bow will notify the player of the charges remaining at increments of 500 charges. It will also notify the player when 100 and 50 charges remain.

Un-charging the bow returns all essence to the player's inventory.

Uses[edit | edit source]

Because of its potential to deal up to 233% of its standard max hit per shot, the venator bow is highly efficient at killing tightly-packed enemies in multicombat areas. Additionally, the arrows' tendency to bounce into nearby non-aggressive targets can help draw aggro, requiring less attention from the player. As such, the bow can be considered a lower cost-per-hour alternative to barraging or chinchompas. Popular spots to use it include abyssal demons and mutated bloodvelds in the Catacombs of Kourend.

The bow is ineffective in single-combat areas, and on most enemies with a size of 3x3 or larger (such as greater demons), since the bounce radius of 2 tiles makes it impossible for arrows to bounce from one large enemy to another unless stacked. It is very effective against the Grotesque Guardians due to the bosses' very low Defence and being stackable, allowing players to hit Dawn twice if Dusk is on top of her.

The bow is effective in killing enemies that drop champion's scrolls.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • "Venator", in Latin, means "hunter".
  • The charged venator bow is currently the only bow in-game to show its bow string when wielded.
  • When pitched in the poll blog, the item's name was "Bloodletter bow". This was because the name of the quest and its rewards were polled by the development team as a "Mysterious Quest", where they wished to keep the Zarosian nature of the rewards a secret.
    • The true name of the quest and its rewards from the boss were then revealed in the Winter Summit 2022 a month later.
  • In RuneScape, venators acted as scouts, hunters and spies for the Zarosian Army.
