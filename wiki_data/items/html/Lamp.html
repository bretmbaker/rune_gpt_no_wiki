The lamp is a reward from several random events, that differs from those awarded by quests. While these too cannot be traded or banked, rubbing one gives the option of adding experience to any skill a player chooses. The amount given is ten times their skill level of choice (e.g. a skill at level 21 nets 210 experience).

Guaranteed[edit | edit source]

  • Count Check (players must be using a Jagex Account and a Bank PIN)
  • Genie
  • Kiss the frog (acquired by exchanging excess tokens to Thessalia, regardless of how many pieces of the Royal frog costume are owned)
  • Beekeeper

With a complete set[edit | edit source]

  • Drill Demon (only once all pieces of the camouflage outfit and all Drill Demon emotes are owned)
  • Freaky Forester (only once all pieces of the lederhosen outfit are owned)
  • Gravedigger (only once all pieces of the zombie outfit and all zombie emotes are owned)
  • Mime (only once all pieces of the mime outfit and all mime emotes are owned)

On the drop table[edit | edit source]

  • Prison Pete
  • Quiz Master (through a Mystery box)

Using lamps[edit | edit source]

After an icon is selected, clicking 'Confirm' at the bottom grants the player experience in the chosen skill.

It should be noted, members must be on a members' world to select member skills. The Construction option cannot be selected unless the player owns a house. Runecraft and Herblore cannot be selected until the completion of Rune Mysteries and Druidic Ritual respectively.

Most players use lamps on skills too time consuming or expensive to train, like Agility, or Construction. Combat skills are seen as a poor use of the lamp, unless one is a combat pure, as lamps allow pures to gain combat experience without training Hitpoints. Skillers sometimes use lamps to train Slayer without raising their combat. A player may hold onto the lamp until they gain a level, especially if they are close to one. This is because it will give slightly more experience (10 more per increase) at a higher level. However, retaining the lamp will take up inventory space, as it cannot be banked, and this can be counterproductive when training some skills. Although it can take some time, it is possible to gain multiple Genie events lamps.

Another common reason for a player keeping a lamp would be for the ease of reaching celebrated levels like a 99. This makes the level-up significantly easier and allows the player to have a much wider choice of level-up locations. An example might be a player achieving level 99 in any skill in their player-owned house by using the lamp to obtain the final experience required to level.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
31 July 2024
(update)

Added XP lamps to the rewards from the Beekeeper random event.

26 June 2024
(update)

A lamp can now also be obtained by exchanging excess frog tokens with Thessalia.

23 August 2023
(update)

Random events with costume rewards would commonly give 500 coins to a player who already had the full outfit. They now give XP lamps instead of those coins. The lamps are identical to the Genie's lamp, giving 10x of your level in the skill. The Quizmaster's Mystery Box and Prison Pete may give such a lamp instead of coins too.

26 January 2022
(update)

The "Drop" option was replaced with a "Destroy" option.

21 March 2019
(update)

The item's value was increased from 1 to 200.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • It would take exactly 15,057 genie lamps to level a skill from level 1 to 99 and another 188,855 to reach 200 mil exp for a total of 203,912 from 0 to 200 mil exp.
  • It takes the same number of lamps to get from level 1 to 2, as it does to get from level 39 to 40.
  • Level 10 experience of 100 gives the highest percentage (49%) of the difference to the next level. The levels 5 to 18 give 40% or more, levels 4 to 23 give 30% or more, levels 2 to 30 give 20% or more and levels 1 to 40 give 10% or more.
  • When trying to bank a lamp it will say "A magical force prevents you from banking this item", and the lamp will stay in your inventory.
  • The lamp can be stored in death collection at Zulrah and Vorkath.
