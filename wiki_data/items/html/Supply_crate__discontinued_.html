A supply crate was a reward item given for subduing the Wintertodt after earning at least 500 points. It was replaced by the Reward Cart, but existing supply crates were not deleted and can still be opened or kept. It can reward many useful items for skilling such as ores, logs, herbs, seeds, gems, and fish. The supply crate also gives the opportunity for items only obtainable through defeating the Wintertodt, such as the tome of fire, pyromancer outfit, and the phoenix pet.

Opening it will give players two rolls on the reward table. For every 500 points earned after the initial 500, another reward is added to the crate. With 13,500 points or above, players will receive 28 reward rolls, the maximum from any single supply crate. Beyond this point, earning extra points does not increase the number of rewards from a supply crate.

Players can exchange excess Wintertodt-unique items (excluding burnt pages and the phoenix) with Ignisia for an extra supply crate or 50 burnt pages. While it is not possible to receive unique items from an extra supply crate, the player may still receive a dragon axe. Tomes of fire may also be exchanged for 250 burnt pages. It is recommended for ironmen to keep 3 warm gloves and 3 bruma torches since this is the maximum and any further rolls for the two will result in a roll for a magic seed or torstol seeds. Players without account restrictions may wish to exchange them for burnt pages instead, as the combined market value of the pages is often higher than that of the seeds.

Drop mechanic[edit | edit source]

Points earned between the 500-point milestones will provide players a chance to receive an additional reward equivalent to the progress towards the next milestone. For every 5 points past 500 points player gets 1% extra chance to get additional loot. For example:

  • 500 points = 2 rolls.
  • 505 points = 2 rolls + 1% chance of an extra roll.
  • 750 points = 2 rolls + 50% chance of an extra roll.
  • 1000 points = 3 rolls.
  • 1005 points = 3 rolls + 1% chance of an extra roll.
  • 1500 points = 4 rolls.


Material quality[edit | edit source]

Points earned do not affect the quality of each drop, only the number of rolls on the drop table. If a material drop is selected (herbs, ores, fish, gems, seeds, and logs), the chances of obtaining a higher quality drop depends upon players' level in its associated skill; Herblore, Mining, Fishing, Crafting, Farming, and Woodcutting respectively.[1] At the highest point, low levelled materials are removed from the drop table. While players with low levels in those skills can get rare materials, the drop rate is very low, but will increase with that skill level.

Because the rewards are determined when opening the crate,[2] players can choose to keep them closed until their relevant skills are at a higher level, allowing them to receive rarer materials and supplies from the crate when they eventually do open them. Do note that supply crates do not stack in a bank; as such, storing too many supply crates may consume too much bank space.

Reward rolls[edit | edit source]

The supply crate uses a special mechanic to determine items obtained. When the crate is opened, a drop will be rolled for until the roll results in a success, as described in the table below.[3]

Note that the success rate is the rate per roll, and players are given at least two rolls per crate, depending on the points gained at the end of the fight.

For example, a player opens the crate and rolls for a dragon axe. If successful, the player will receive a dragon axe. If unsuccessful, then the player will then roll for a phoenix. If that roll is successful, they will receive the phoenix and the drop rolls end there. If unsuccessful, the player will roll for the tome of fire, then the warm gloves, and so on. If none of the unique drop rolls are successful, then the player will receive an item from the normal drop table, which contains burnt pages, seeds, logs, etc. It is possible to receive more than 3 sets of the Pyromancer outfit (Garb, Hood, Robe, Boots).

Roll Table Successful roll Effective Rate[d 1]
1 Dragon axe 1/10,000 1/10,000
2 Phoenix 1/5,000 1/5,000.5
3 Tome of fire 1/1,000 1/1,000.3
4 Warm gloves[d 2] 1/150 1/150.2
5 Bruma torch[d 3] 1/150 1/151.2
6 Pyromancer outfit[d 4] 1/150 1/152.2
  1. ^ Chance of receiving this drop (counting failures of previous tables)
  2. ^ If the player already has three pairs of warm gloves and it is successfully rolled, a magic seed is given.[4]
  3. ^ If the player already has three bruma torches and it is successfully rolled, some torstol seeds are given.[4]
  4. ^ The piece given is the piece the player has least of, ties are broken in this order: Garb, hood, robe, and boots. If the player has the Pyromancer robe stashed away, it will not count as owning it when rolling for a new piece.

Logs[edit | edit source]

There is about a 1/9 chance to drop on the logs subtable. The exact drop rates on this subtable are based on the player's Woodcutting level; a higher level player is more likely to receive magic logs than a lower level player. All of the logs can be received with a much lower woodcutting level than it would take to cut them from trees; most notably this allows Ironmen to get Magic logs up to 25 levels earlier.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
9 October 2024
(update | poll)

Supply crates were replaced with the Reward Cart.

21 June 2023
(update | poll)

The drop rate of phoenixes has been increased by fifteen times if players have 200 million experience in Firemaking.

8 April 2020
(update | poll)
  • The rate at which burnt pages show up in Wintertodt supply crates has increased by 50%.
25 April 2019
(update)

The supply crate can now be opened even if inventory does not have enough space for all potential rewards. The reward cap of 28 was not changed.

6 October 2016
(update)
  • Rewards from supply crates will now be dependent on player's levels in various skills.
  • Full sets of the Pyromancer outfit can be exchanged with Ignisia for a supply crate which provides four loot rolls.
