A reward casket (elite) is received after completing the final clue of an elite clue scroll trail, and contains the reward for the trail.

While players can only have one of each type of clue scroll in their possession, they can possess unlimited amounts of reward caskets, and are eligible to receive clue scrolls again once it is obtained.

Rewards[edit | edit source]

Elite clues generate a minimum of 4 rewards and a maximum of 6, granting an average of 5 rewards per casket. The rewards consist of generic items, shared treasure trail items, and items that are unique for elite clues. Additionally, players have a 1/5 chance to receive a master clue scroll upon opening an elite casket - this does not take up one of the 4-6 reward slots.

There is also a 1/35 chance of the reward casket being a mimic, which can be taken to the strange casket in Watson's house to fight The Mimic. Players are only eligible to receive mimics if they have spoken to the strange casket to enable the chance of a reward casket turning into a mimic. If a player attempts to open an elite reward casket for the first time before speaking to the strange casket, they will be asked whether they want a chance of a Mimic boss fight or not, and wanting it will result in the chatbox directing players to the strange casket.

All droprates (except for the Mimic rewards) are confirmed by Mod Tide.[1][2] The listed rarities are per roll.

The average loot roll is worth 83,205.09 and average elite clues are worth 416,025.47, without considering the value from potential master clues. The 1/5 chance of receiving a master clue scroll adds another 175,501.18, for a total expected value per clue of 591,526.65.

Date Changes
11 May 2022
(update | poll)

Changed the rarity of 3rd Age items from Elite Clues to fit proportionally between Hard and Master Clues.

15 August 2019
(update | poll)

The locks on the reward casket have been changed to reflect the tier of Treasure Trail it comes from.

11 April 2019
 Hotfix

The chance of an elite reward casket being a mimic was changed from 1/80 to 1/35.[5]

11 April 2019
(update | poll)

All Treasure Trails caskets were changed to be stackable.

6 July 2016
(update | poll)

All of the lower tier clues are getting many new additions to the drop tables.

3 July 2014
(update)

Extended antifire potions rewarded from elite treasure trails are now noted.

26 June 2014
(update | poll)

The Rangers' tunic are now available as a reward from elite reward caskets.

12 June 2014
(update | poll)

There are over 180 new items available to be discovered by solving clue scrolls including third age weaponry, new outfits and a host of other items. There are new rewards and brand new clues for every level of treasure trail.
