Escape crystals are a consumable teleport item that can be equipped in the ring slot. It can be purchased from Rick in the 1st floor[UK]2nd floor[US] of the Wizards' Tower, as well as from the Mysterious Stranger in Ver Sinhaza, for 75,000 coins.

The crystal acts similarly to a respawn teleport, teleporting players away from various activities, which Hardcore ironmen/group ironmen may consider as dangerous upon death:

  • Barbarian Assault
  • Chambers of Xeric
  • The Inferno
  • Pest Control
  • Theatre of Blood
  • TzHaar-Ket-Rak's Challenges
  • Tombs of Amascut

Players can also use the crystal on their group members to activate them, allowing teleportation of teammates out of danger in an instant. To enable this, players must right-click the crystal and select "Configure", then choose the first option. Both players must have a crystal for this to work, and both crystals will be consumed in the process.

Additionally, the crystal can be configured to auto-activate upon taking damage (including zeros) after a specified time of inactivity (ranging from 2 to 300 seconds) via the "Configure" option; in this case, inactivity is defined as any time the player is not typing, clicking/tapping within the game, moving/zooming the camera, or moving the mouse[1]. For example, if players configure the timer to 30 seconds, they will automatically be teleported away from the aforementioned activities upon taking damage after being inactive for at least 30 seconds. This function will apply in The Gauntlet if the crystal was in the player's inventory upon entry, and is configured to auto-activate.

The crystal will NOT activate while the player is moving. If the inactive time is reached at any point during movement, the crystal will activate when the player stops moving.

The crystal can also be equipped and toggled to act in the same manner as a ring of life, although this will cause the crystal to be unable to teleport out from the aforementioned activities, so players must have it unequipped should they wish to be able to do so in those activities.

Furthermore when engaging in wilderness content the teleport function will work up to level 30, putting it in the same category as the royal seed pod and dragonstone jewellery.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
26 March 2025
(update)

Logging out whilst in the Theatre of Blood or Tombs of Amascut while having an escape crystal in your inventory will now consume the crystal and teleport you outside of the raid on login.

13 November 2024
(update)

The escape crystal is now disabled on the permanent Deadman Mode world.

19 July 2023

Camera zooms, camera pans, and mouse movement now count as "activity" for the escape crystal's auto-AFK mechanic.

28 June 2023
(update)
  • There is now a visual and textual indication to tell players when the crystal is active or deactivated.
  • An "Auto-afk" right-click option has been added.
  • The "Activate" option has been replaced by "Teleport".
21 June 2023
(update | poll)
  • A "Wear" option was added, allowing the escape crystal to be worn in the ring slot.
  • The "Toggle" option was changed to "Configure".
    • Players can now configure the escape crystal to automatically teleport them after a customisable period of inactivity.
    • Players can now configure the escape crystal to act as a ring of life when worn.
11 October 2022
(update)

Using the crystal on another player in the Tombs of Amascut no longer consumes two escape crystals.

2 March 2022
(update)
  • The item's name was changed from "Verzik's crystal shard" to "Escape crystal".
  • The examine text was changed; previously, it was "The only teleport that overcomes the preventative magicks of the Theatre of Blood."
4 August 2021
(update | poll)

Players can now use the crystal on their party members to activate them, allowing teleportation of teammates out of the raid in an instant. To enable this, right-click a crystal and select "Toggle". Both players must have a crystal for this to work, and both crystals will be consumed in the process.
