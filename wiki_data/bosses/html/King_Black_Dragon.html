The King Black Dragon, commonly referred to as KBD, is a three-headed dragon located in his lair, accessible only through the deep Wilderness. Although the lair itself is not in the Wilderness, KBD is still considered a Wilderness boss by designation, as players must enter the Wilderness to fight him. The King Black Dragon has a combat level of 276, which makes him one of the strongest dragons in Old School RuneScape. Players should not take this foe lightly, and they should always take an anti-dragon shield as his dragonfire breath is very deadly without protection. The King Black Dragon can also be killed as an alternative for a black dragon Slayer task, except when assigned by Krystilia.

Although he will always fight back when attacked, he has gotten bored over the centuries from adventurers attacking him, as he revealed to his good friend Bob the cat.

While the King Black Dragon's lair is entered from within the Wilderness, his lair itself is not part of the Wilderness, so players cannot attack each other in his lair. Completion of the Wilderness Diary also does not note his bone drops.

Players can pay 50,000 coins to open an instanced version of his lair (right-click Private option on lever), where it will be inaccessible by other players. This fee can be deducted directly from the bank; players do not need to carry coins on them to create the instance. Items dropped by the King Black Dragon in this instance will disappear after 1800 seconds (30 minutes), however items dropped by the player will disappear after the typical 180 seconds (3 minutes).

Getting there[edit | edit source]

Warning: The entrance to the King Black Dragon Lair (aka KBD Lair) is located in the Wilderness; watch out for player killers!

Note: After climbing down the ladder near the lesser demons, players must pull a lever surrounded by poisonous spiders to access the lair.

  • The burning amulet teleports the player to the Lava Maze just south-east of the fenced area containing the entrance to the lair. This is the fastest and most reliable method of getting there.
  • Wilderness Obelisks can be used, but be wary that using them will make encountering high-level monsters or PKers more likely. Players who have completed the hard Wilderness Diary can choose the obelisk they wish to teleport to, namely the obelisk closest to the lair at level 44 Wilderness. The nearest obelisk can be accessed if the player teleports to the Corporeal Beast area via a games necklace, exits the cave, and runs south-east to the obelisk and then south-east again.
  • From Edgeville, players can bring an axe to make a waka canoe (requires 57 Woodcutting) at the canoe station and take it to the Wilderness. Upon arrival, the player will have to trek westward past some green dragons on the southern end of the Lava Maze before going slightly north-west toward the western part of the maze.
  • North-west of Ardougne Castle, there's a lever that takes the player to the Deserted Keep. From there, after slashing the webs, head southward to a long fence and follow it either east or west to an opening. If following it east, beware of the Chaos Elemental. Once out, trek westward past the northern end of the Lava Maze before going slightly south-west toward the western part of the maze.
  • The Ice Plateau or Ghorrock Teleport is among the fastest ways to get there. After teleporting, run south-east.
  • The Arceuus spellbook's Cemetery Teleport teleports players to the Forgotten Cemetery, just south.

Fight overview[edit | edit source]

Main article: King Black Dragon/Strategies

In addition to his damaging attacks, the King Black Dragon can also poison the player and reduce the player's stats. It is strongly recommended to bring anti-poison (Antidote++ is preferred) and stat-restoring potions such as super restores.

His dragonfire is significantly stronger than normal dragonfire, with a max hit of 65 with no protection, 15 with the anti-dragon shield or dragonfire shield, and is completely blocked with both the shield and antifire potion. Because the max damage is so high, the player should always have one of these shields equipped.

In addition to his regular dragonfire, the King Black Dragon has three unblockable breath attacks: Poison, Shock, and Freeze. A super antifire potion will only nullify all damage from the regular dragonfire, and the special breath will still deal up to its full 50 damage. With the anti-dragon shield or equivalent equipped, they only deal up to 10 damage. Protect from Magic does not protect against or reduce damage from dragonfire when an anti-dragon shield or equivalent is equipped. However, if no anti-dragon shield is equipped, Protect from Magic will reduce the max hit of these attacks from 50 to 20.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • King Black Dragon appears on one of the seven Postbag from the Hedge stamps.
  • Engaging in combat with the King Black Dragon while not having any form of dragonfire protection gives unique messages in the player's chatbox when they are harmed by the various types of dragonfire:
    • Freezing: You're chilled to the bone by the dragon's icy breath!
    • Poison: You're badly scalded by the dragon's toxic breath!
    • Shocking: You're zapped by the dragon's shocking breath!
    • Burning: You're horribly burnt by the dragon's fiery breath!
  • The King Black Dragon can talk, as seen in A Tail of Two Cats. During the final cutscene, when he is visited by Bob the cat, they have a conversation before being interrupted by R4ng3rno0b889.
  • The King Black Dragon had a festive override wearing a santa hat, purple partyhat, and green partyhat during the 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024 Christmas events.
  • The King Black Dragon was the strongest monster in Runescape Classic and retained the title until the introduction of Runescape 2's first new boss, the Kalphite Queen.
