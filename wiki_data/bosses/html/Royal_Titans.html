The Royal Titans are a boss encounter consisting of the two giants Eldric the Ice King and Branda the Fire Queen. Rulers over their domains in the Asgarnian Ice Dungeon and beneath the Karamja Volcano respectively, Branda is set on expanding her territory and has begun clashing with Eldric.

The fight is intended to be fought by two players with a combat level of at least 90 with emphasis on gear switches; while players may solo them, the fight is much quicker with a partner. A second player can join an instance until one of the titans is damaged below 450 Hitpoints. Unlike the moss giant boss Bryophyta and the hill giant boss Obor, no additional items are needed to fight the Royal Titans.

The fight takes place on a strip of land between the two Titans, who each have 600 Hitpoints. They will periodically walk towards land, allowing them to be attacked with melee. At other times, players must attack them with ranged or magic. Once one of the Titans reaches below 35% of their health, both Titans will teleport onto land, charging a powerful attack that can be avoided by standing on the tile where the energy converges. If one of the Titans is defeated before the other, the remaining Titan will have their mechanics enhanced, such as their melee attacks piercing through prayer. This makes it important to keep both Titans' health low enough before defeating them to make the fight less of a hassle.

Players who die fighting the boss will have their gravestone placed outside the entrance, which follows standard death mechanics.

Group ironman players will lose their prestige upon entering the encounter with a player outside of their group.

Location[edit | edit source]

The Royal Titans can be accessed in the Asgarnian Ice Dungeon (fairy ring code AIQ). The entrance tunnel to the bosses can be found in the western part of the dungeon. With level 60 Agility, a tunnel shortcut just west of the dungeon ladder can be used for faster access to the boss. Furthermore, a giantsoul amulet can instead be used to directly teleport outside of the bosses' entrance tunnel.

A scoreboard by the tunnel shows statistics related to the Royal Titans.

    • Rewards[edit | edit source]

    Upon the Titans' defeat, they will fall to the ground and players can choose to loot either Eldric or Branda; both have similar but different standard drops, with their unique drops being distinct, allowing players to put their focus on a specific drop if desired.

    Players have three options when looting them:

    • Loot - makes two primary rolls on the drop table.
    • Sacrifice - forfeits drops in return for doubling the pet drop rate
    • Take pages - rewards desiccated pages, which can be converted into burnt, soaked, or soiled pages at their respective plinths. Tertiary drops (clue scrolls and the pet) are still rolled.[1]

    If a player does not loot a Titan before they respawn 30 ticks (18 seconds) later, their loot for the kill will be lost.

    Contribution[edit | edit source]

    The drop rates and quantities listed below assume the Royal Titans are killed by one player.

    With two players, the quantities of regular items and the drop rates of unique items scale linearly, proportional to the damage dealt by that player.

    • For example, a player who deals 50% of the damage to the titans will roll twice for 140-160 coal, and a player who deals 100% of the damage will roll twice for 280-320 coal.
    • Similarly, a player who deals 50% of the damage will have a 1/150 chance of receiving a fire element staff crown, and a player dealing 100% of the damage will have a 1/75 chance.
    • Contribution is calculated as a whole number percent of damage dealt to the bosses (including recoil damage). A player who deals more than 0% but less than 1% of the total damage (from 1 to 11 damage) will still roll for two items on the main table, but only receive 1 of each item. Above 1% damage the quantities scale linearly.
      • If choosing the "Take pages" option, it is possible to receive a single desiccated page having dealt as little as 72 damage, but a page is not guaranteed until dealing 120 damage.

    The drop rates of the pet and clue scrolls do not depend on contribution.[2]

    Changes[edit | edit source]

    Date Changes
    18 February 2025
    (update)
    • Combat Achievements were added for the bosses. As criteria for Royal Titan Speed-Runner was tracked prior to this update, the task is automatically completed upon opening the Combat Achievements interface.
    • The health threshold before players are blocked from joining an active fight has been lowered from 510 to 450 Hitpoints.
    • A "Quick-start" option has been added to the entrance.
    • The second hit from the twinflame staff will now apply the reduced attack speed for the next attack if it kills off an elemental.
    12 February 2025
    (update)
    • The enrage slam animation is no longer delayed by a tick on certain occasions.
    • A bug has been fixed where the titan’s body disappeared upon death, its base persisted after respawning, and ice remained in the arena for the next fight.
    • Teleporting out of the Royal Titans boss arena will now nullify any incoming damage to prevent death.
    • The fire and ice elemental's hitboxes have been scaled down and no longer take up so much space.
    • On sacrifice, if a player does not receive the pet, a message will appear in the chatbox to say: "You don't feel lucky during your sacrifice".

    Trivia[edit | edit source]

    • The Royal Titans have a much higher combat level than the other giant bosses; both have a combat level of 350, surpassing Obor's combat level of 106 and Bryophyta's combat level of 128.
    • When the Royal Titans are defeated, their combat level drops from 350 to 125.
    • They are the only giant bosses to have a pet drop; this is likely due to the fact that they are members-only bosses, whereas Obor and Bryophyta can be killed by free-to-play players.
    • Despite being giants, they do not drop the giant champion scroll.
