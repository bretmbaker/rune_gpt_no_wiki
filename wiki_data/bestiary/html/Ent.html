Ents are monsters found in the dungeon beneath the Woodcutting Guild and in the forests north and east of the Chaos Temple. Ents are found on members' worlds exclusively.

Upon death, the ent's trunk will fall and remain on the ground for 1 minute. These remains can be chopped to give several sets of noted logs according to the player's Woodcutting level. Players also have a chance to receive a bird nest while chopping the ent trunk. While cutting the trunk, players get 25 experience per set of logs received no matter the type. Completing the medium Wilderness Diary increases the chance of successfully chopping ents in the Wilderness by 15%.[1] Ents can be safespotted by moving 1-3 tiles adjacent to a tree and using a ranged weapon, halberd, or magic attack.

Chopping the trunks requires an axe that the player has the Woodcutting level to use. The type of logs that can be received is determined by a player's base Woodcutting level, with higher levels giving better logs. The chance to successfully receive logs is determined by the player's current Woodcutting level (boosted or drained), with higher levels increasing success chance. The distribution of log types is determined by the axe used, with better axes providing a greater amount of higher tier logs.

While Ents do drop slayer's enchantments, while on a Wilderness slayer task, they do not drop looting bags or Larran's keys.[2]

Ent trunks are only choppable by the player who originally killed the ent.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
2 June 2016
(update | poll)
  • Ents have been added to the Woodcutting Guild in a dungeon.
  • The rate at which you receive logs from Ents within the wilderness has been doubled.
5 March 2015
(update | poll)

You now cut logs 15% faster from ents if you have completed the Wilderness Medium Diary.

27 March 2014
(update | poll)

After you chop the log left from a dead ent, you will now be able to receive a bird nest as a drop.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Even if the player already has a full inventory including notes of every log type possible from ents, attempting to chop an ent trunk will yield the usual message, "Your inventory is too full to carry any more logs."
  • The Ent's examine text is the exact same as the Giant Ent from RuneScape.
