Aviansie are avian monsters found in both the God Wars Dungeon and Wilderness God Wars Dungeon. Like other creatures within the dungeon, they can be found fighting for their god Armadyl, engaging in combat against the followers of Zamorak.

The ability to be assigned aviansie can be purchased for 80 slayer reward points with the Watch the birdie slayer reward. Once unlocked, they can be toggled off. Though, in order to toggle them back on, they must be purchased again for 80 slayer points or removed from the assignments list through the use of the block slots.

Unlike most other followers, however, aviansie have an increased sense of danger. Even if they are in combat with another monster, if the player attempts to attack them, the aviansie will start attacking the player immediately; this trait is shared by all Armadylean monsters.

As they are airborne, they are immune to being directly targeted by melee attacks except for salamanders. They can be targeted by Ranged or Magic attacks. If the player attempts to attack an aviansie with a melee weapon, except for a salamander, the message The Aviansie is flying too high for you to attack using melee. will appear in their chatbox, and the aviansie will become aggressive and start attacking the player if it hasn't already. A lone exception to this rule is indirectly attacking the aviansie through weapons such as the scythe of vitur or the special attack of dinh's bulwark on a primary target, provided that the aviansie is in range to be hit first.

Aviansie are known for their frequent adamantite bar drops. The aviansie in the God Wars Dungeon will drop them unnoted unless the player has completed the Hard Fremennik tasks, whereas those in the Wilderness drop them noted. The latter is much more dangerous however due to the risk of player killers, and these aviansie do not drop looting bags despite being in the Wilderness.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Avian means "of or relating to birds".
  • Aviansie come in two types; female aviansie who wear helmets and have a lower-cut platebody, and males aviansie who don't wear helmets.
    • Stat-wise, lower-levelled female aviansie have a lower Defence level than their male counterparts, but the moderate and high-level ones possess a greater Defence and Ranged level than their corresponding variant.
