Fiyr Shades are undead spectral creatures that lurk behind the silver door in the Mort'ton shade catacombs dungeon. They initially appear as black moving shadows on the ground and then rise from the depths to attack. On occasion they have also been known to leave their catacombs to attack trekkers and ramblers in the town above their dark burial place. Their remains can be cremated with magic pyre logs or redwood pyre logs as part of the Shades of Mort'ton minigame, requiring a Firemaking level of 80 and 95 respectively.

As with all shades in Mort'ton, Fiyr shades possess 2 traits you should be aware of:

  1. A unique trait in which their failed accuracy rolls do not count as combat; therefore actions usually restricted during combat can still be performed should this occur. Note that this also affects auto-retaliate, however they can still successfully hit a 0.
  2. A 1/20 chance to temporarily lower a player's Strength by 1 on a successful hit that inflicts damage, which can stack.[1].

As Fiyr Shades are undead, they are susceptible to the effects of Crumble Undead and the Salve amulet. They are also weak to the Gadderhammer, although a dragon scimitar deals more damage on average.

Treasure Trails[edit | edit source]

  • Killing a Fiyr Shade inside the Mort'ton shade catacombs is required to complete a master clue scroll skill challenge assigned by Sherlock. This can be most easily accomplished via the steps in Money making guide/Killing fiyr shades.
  • Cremating a set of fiyr remains is required to complete a master clue scroll skill challenge assigned by Sherlock.
