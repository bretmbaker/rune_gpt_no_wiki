Kurask are Slayer monsters that require a Slayer level of 70 to damage, as well as usage of leaf-bladed weapons, broad bolts, broad arrows, amethyst broad bolts, or Magic Dart. Kurask are immune to poison and venom. Kurask are also immune to damage from a dwarf multicannon. They can, however, take burning damage from weapons such as burning claws.

Their attacks are relatively inaccurate due to their low attack level, but they can hit somewhat high if their attacks manage to land. Additionally, they have fast health regeneration, healing one hitpoint every five game ticks.

Although kurask are invulnerable to any damage from Saradomin godsword, ancient godsword and toxic blowpipe special attacks, the healing effect will still activate as if damage had been dealt, making them useful as special attack weapons during extended trips. The ancient mace and eldritch nightmare staff special attacks likewise restore Prayer points based on what damage would have been dealt. The Guthan's equipment set effect does not restore health.

These monsters, along with Turoth, drop the leaf-bladed sword. They are also known for their light mystic robe top and kurask head drops, as well as being the only monster to drop the leaf-bladed battleaxe.

Bug:
Kurasks and turoths are currently vulnerable to damage from ghostly thralls (magic) and skeletal thralls (ranged). They are still immune to damage from zombie thralls (melee).

Drops[edit | edit source]

Drop rates estimated based on 480,493 samples from the Drop Rate Project, unless otherwise cited.

The average Kurask kill is worth 5,779.44.

The average Kurask kill while on a Konar task is worth 7,120.43.

The average Kurask kill in Iorwerth Dungeon is worth 6,457.00 due to the additional chance of receiving a crystal shard as a tertiary drop. This value for crystal shards assumes that they are made into divine super combat potions. For a Konar task in the Iorwerth Dungeon, the average Kurask kill is worth 7,798.00

As they drop 3 herbs at a time, it may be worth considering bringing a herb sack to prolong slayer trips.

Date Changes

(update)

The amount of crystal shards obtained from slayer monsters in the Iorwerth Dungeon has increased from 1 to 3-5.

(update)

The drop of 1020 limpwurt roots (added in the previous drop table rebalance) was changed to a single limpwurt seed to balance the influx of limpwurt roots coming into the game.

(update | poll)

The drop table was improved to include more valuable drops and increase quantity of existing drops as well as drop some as banknotes.

(update | poll)

The leaf-bladed sword is now dropped by Kurask and Turoth.
