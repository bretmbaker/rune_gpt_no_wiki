Wyrms are draconic creatures found in the lower level of the Karuulm Slayer Dungeon in Mount Karuulm requiring level 62 Slayer to kill. Players can get there quickly by using the fairy ring CIR located south of the mountain. Players can also directly teleport to Mount Karuulm with Rada’s blessing 3 or 4. As they are only found within the volcano, players must wear the boots of stone, boots of brimstone or granite boots to protect themselves from the extreme heat of the dungeon floor. Players that have completed the Elite Kourend & Kebos Diary do not need to wear this footwear to avoid being rapidly burned.

Killing a Wyrm is required for the Hard Kourend & Kebos Diary and the Easy Combat Achievements.

Wyrms were an ancient race of dragons, said to be capable of destroying cities and armies on their own. They slowly disappeared due to their heavy involvement in the God Wars. The current Wyrms in the dungeon are an attempted recreation of the powerful Wyrms of old by a Dragonkin named Karuulm.

Wyrms do not require any form of dragonfire protection. When encountered, they will be slithering on the ground. However, once attacked, they will levitate off the ground and attack the player. Wyrms attack with both Melee and Magic; however, if attacked from a distance, they will only use their magic attack. This makes the Ranged attack style while using a form of magic protection, such as Protect from Magic, or armour with high magic defence bonuses like dragonhide, a viable method for killing Wyrms. They are also immune to both venom and poison.

They are the only monster to drop the dragon sword and dragon harpoon.

Wyrms are vulnerable to the effects of Dragonbane weapons, which includes the dragon hunter lance, dragon hunter crossbow and dragon hunter wand. A dwarf multicannon cannot be set up in the area as a message will appear "The electricity here is too powerful for you to set up a cannon here".

Players on a wyrm Slayer task can alternatively kill wyrmlings in Neypotzli Dungeon, they reside in the earthbound caverns subsection (unless task is location locked. ie. Slayer master Konar quo Maten). They are lower level variants with easy access to prayer restore, food and free super combat potions nearby. They however only drop wyrmling bones making them much worse for profit.

Safespotting[edit | edit source]

If the player is on a Slayer task, any idle wyrms on the eastern alcove of the second island can be attacked and then lured to the corner to trap them, allowing players to range them at a specific spot while staying out of their magic attack range of 6 squares. Wyrms will return to their idle state after being unable to retaliate for several hits, and may walk out of the safespot area in the process. This can cause the player to run after the wyrm and start taking damage. For this reason it is recommended to pay some attention even while using this method. A player can ensure more idle time by attacking with a weapon that has an attack speed of 5 ticks (e.g. magic longbow or rune crossbow in with their attack style set to rapid). If timed correctly, the attack will hit the wyrm as soon as its "re-idling" animation ends, preventing it from moving away and undoing the safespot.

Furthermore, if the wyrm manages to move away and enter the platform's northern "corridor", the player can avoid having to reposition it by setting up a safespot along the second island's northwestern edge, 6 squares from the corner.

However, when using the north eastern alcove safespot then the superior version, the Shadow Wyrm, will not have room to spawn as it is a larger 5x5. There is a tile near the middle of the northern wall outside of the alcove that should be used if one wishes to have superior versions be able to spawn in as shown in the attached files. To setup this safespot, attack a wyrm when it is in the eastern alcove and run to the shown tile, the more north and east the wyrm is the higher chance of instant success. You may have to run to the back of the northern alcove to lure the wyrm's character box past the western wall of the northern alcove if the wyrm doesn't automatically get stuck on said western wall. Note that the Shadow Wyrm cannot be safespotted.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Wyrms, from the Old English word meaning "serpent", are legendary dragons in European mythology. Unlike the Wyrm's in-game model, wyrms were described as legless in early Germanic sagas.
  • Being a wingless, 4-legged draconic creature, the Wyrm resembles depictions of the Lung or Chinese dragon
  • According to Juna, Wyrms took part in the God Wars, and the strength of dragons pale in comparison. It is unknown who the wyrms served in the war.
