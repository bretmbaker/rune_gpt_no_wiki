Barrows equipment

Barrows equipment refers to a number of sets of combat equipment found in the Barrows minigame . There are six sets of Barrows equipment: four for warriors , one for rangers , and one for mages . Each set has four pieces: a two-handed weapon (except for Ahrim's Staff , which after an update was changed to be a one-handed weapon like other staves in an effort to buff the weapon), a helmet, a torso piece, and legwear. When all four pieces for a particular brother are equipped, a special set effect is granted, which is different for each set. Additionally, the amulet of the damned enhances the set effects while equipped. Barrows sets cannot be made using the Smithing skill .

The Barrows gloves , which also resemble a piece of Barrows armour, are not obtained through the Barrows minigame and instead can be purchased after full completion of the Recipe for Disaster quest.

Degrading and repairing [ edit | edit source ]

Barrows equipment degrades as it is used. Equipment received from the Barrows minigame is fully repaired and can be used for 15 hours of combat until it degrades completely and requires repair before it can be used again. Barrows armour degrades even if the player is using protection prayers or not taking damage. Barrows equipment does not suffer in performance as it degrades, but will lose all stat bonuses when it becomes fully degraded. Fully repaired Barrows items start out with no numbers in their names. It will become item name 100 the first time it's used in combat. Eventually it will degrade to item name 75 , then 50 , then 25 and finally 0 at which point the armour will be unusable until repaired (armour that reaches 0 whilst being worn will stay worn until removed, but cannot be reequipped if removed until repaired). Additionally, Barrows equipment may only be traded if fully repaired or fully degraded.

Each quarter consists of 250 degradable points, and cause the item to decay into the next named degradation state once they are fully depleted. A piece of barrows equipment will immediately degrade when the player enters combat, and deduct one more point from the total if the player is still in combat after 90 ticks (54 seconds) have passed. [1]

When dropped, any piece of Barrows equipment will automatically degrade to 0 . Players who attempt to drop Barrows equipment will receive a warning message about this.

There are two ways to repair Barrows armour. Players can talk to any of the NPCs listed below and they will repair the items for a price, or players could repair the pieces themselves using an armour stand in any player-owned house . The equipment can be repaired at any stage, and the cost will be adjusted according to how much it has degraded. The base repair cost of an item is determined by its internal degradation state (out of 1,000), multiplied by 100 coins when repairing a weapon; 90 coins when repairing a body slot item; 80 coins for a leg slot item; and finally 60 coins for a helmet. [2]

The following NPCs can repair the armour:

  • Bob - the axe seller in Lumbridge
  • Tindel Marchant - the weapon and armour repairman at Port Khazard
  • Dunstan - the smith in Burthorpe
  • Squire - in the workshop area of the Void Knights' Outpost
  • Aneirin - the armour seller in Prifddinas
  • Blacksmith - the blacksmith of Civitas illa Fortis

It costs less to repair Barrows equipment at an armour stand compared to paying an NPC. Stat boosts do affect the price of repair. The formula used to calculate the cost at a repair stand is:

Cost A S = Cost N P C × ( 1 − Smithing Level 2 0 0 )

A player with a Smithing level of 60, for instance, would pay 70% of normal cost:

1 − 6 0 2 0 0 = 7 0 1 0 0

Simply put, every 2 Smithing levels reduces the repair cost by 1% when compared to an NPC vendor. At level 99 this equals roughly 50% in savings.

Barrows equipment stacks in bank with the corresponding item degradation , so if a Karil's crossbow has a degradation level of 100, it will stack with another Karil's crossbow if it also has a degradation level of 100. The item with the same degradation level will be taken out with the same amount of hits as before, so if a Karil's crossbow 100 has 180 ticks left before it will go to level 75 degradation, and that same one is in a stack with another crossbow that has 900 ticks before it will degrade further, the one you take out of the stack will be the one with 180 ticks left.

Main article: Ahrim the Blighted's equipment

Dharok the Wretched (Melee) [ edit | edit source ]

Main article: Dharok the Wretched's equipment

70 Defence is required to wear the armour, and 70 Attack and Strength are required to wield the weapon. Dharok's helm has less defence than that of the other melee brothers'. Dharok's is the Strength armour.

Wretched Strength: Attacks do more damage as the player's Hitpoints decrease. The damage bonus per hitpoint increases based on the player's maximum hitpoints. Dharok's maximum potential is unleashed at 99 HP whereof a player can deal up to 98% extra damage when at 1 health point.

Karil the Tainted (Ranged) [ edit | edit source ]

Main article: Karil the Tainted's equipment

70 Defence and 70 Ranged are required to wear the armour, and 70 Ranged is required to wield the weapon. Karil's is the Ranged armour . Note that Karil's has the highest Magic Defence of all the Barrows equipment. It has some of the best magical Defence available in the game, greater than black d'hide and just behind Armadyl ranged gear .

Tainted Shot: Successful ranged attacks have a 25% chance of lowering the enemy's Agility level by 20%.

Verac the Defiled (Melee) [ edit | edit source ]

Main article: Verac the Defiled's equipment

70 Defence is required to wear the armour, and 70 Attack is required to wield the weapon. Verac's helm and plateskirt trade a small amount of Ranged defence for their prayer bonus, while the brassard is significantly weaker than the other brothers' platebodies. Verac's is the Prayer and Attack armour.

Defiler: Attacks have a 25% chance of ignoring any armour, Defence, and protection prayers .

Changes [ edit | edit source ]

Date Changes
8 January 2025
( update )

Fixed an issue where pieces of Barrows kits would break prematurely when attempting to drop them with the 'valuable item' warning active.

18 June 2015
( update )

Degraded barrows equipment, mage arena capes and achievement diary reward items are now also protected on death, outside of PvP, in line with other untradeable items.

Trivia [ edit | edit source ]

  • Upon release, Barrows equipment did not degrade ; it was changed about a week later in an update due to outcry from players claiming it would devalue Dragon equipment .
  • Prior to an update , guthan's chainskirt had transparent sections, similar to ghostly robes .
  • Dharok's set is the only Barrows set that doesn't have an animation after activating its set effect .
  • Despite being followers of Saradomin, Barrows equipment does not provide protection from Saradomin's followers in the God Wars Dungeon (and the Wilderness variant ).
