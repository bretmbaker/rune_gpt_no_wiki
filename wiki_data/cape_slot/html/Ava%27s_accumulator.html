Ava's accumulator is an item awarded to the player by completing the quest Animal Magnetism if the player is above level 50 Ranged (players with lower than 50 Ranged will instead receive Ava's attractor). It is equipped in the cape slot, can pick up a player's ranged ammunition after they fire it, and every 3.5 minutes, will attract an assortment of metal ammunition. The accumulator gives the fourth best Ranged attack bonus for the cape slot.

Like all of Ava's devices, the accumulator will not work when metal torso armour (Ahrim's top included) is worn, as this will cause 'interference'. A list of metal torso armour that does not interfere with Ava's accumulator can be found here.

Ava (found in Draynor Manor) will upgrade the attractor for anyone who obtains 50 Ranged after the quest for a price of 75 steel arrows, along with either 999 coins or an attractor (If you bring both coins and an attractor, the attractor will be used). After that, it can be upgraded to Ava's assembler (upon completion of Dragon Slayer II) for Vorkath's head and 75 mithril arrows, as well as 4,999 coins or an Accumulator.

Upon dying, if it is not an item kept on death, it will be stored in the player's grave until reclaimed. If not reclaimed within an hour, it can be reacquired by speaking with Ava and purchased for 999 coins and 75 steel arrows. Multiple devices may be possessed at the same time.

As with Ava's other devices, Ava's accumulator can be used on both Lanthus in the Castle Wars lobby and Nomad in the Soul Wars lobby to permanently activate the effect in the minigames.

The Ava's accumulator can also be combined with the max cape to change its appearance by turning it into the accumulator max cape.

Lastly, the breakdown of the ammo recovery rate is as listed:

  • Break on impact: 20%
  • Drop onto floor: 8%
  • Recovered automatically: 72%

Metal attraction[edit | edit source]

Approximately every 3.5 minutes, Ava's accumulator will attract an assortment of steel items, provided that the player has moved at least three tiles since the last pickup.[1]

Players can commune with the accumulator to stop it from gathering junk by right-clicking "Commune" on the item while it is worn or in the inventory. The average GE value of these items is 19.60.

Cost saving benefits[edit | edit source]

The ammo retention of the accumulator results in ammunition being fired 3.57 times before it is lost, assuming it is not picked up from the ground. This means it is approximately three and half times cheaper than the price paid. Picking up ammo would make it equal to the Ava's Assembler's 80% ammo retention rate, where each ammunition is fired 5 times before it is destroyed.

Let p(n) be the probability to shoot a single arrow exactly n times and P(n) be the probability to shoot a single arrow at most n times.

P(n)=p(1)+p(2)+⋯+p(n−1)+p(n).

By definition p(1)=1 because a single arrow can always be shot once. Since the recovery rate for a single arrow is 0.72,

P(n)=1+0.72+0.722+⋯+0.72n−1+0.72n=∑k=0n0.72k.

The expected number of shots fired per arrow is then

limn→∞∑k=0n0.72k=11−0.72=3.57.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
21 October 2020
(update)
  • Ava's accumulator is no longer automatically protected upon a non-PvP death.
  • The item now has a "Drop" option instead of "Destroy". Previously, the destroy text was "I can obtain a replacement for this from Ava in Draynor Manor. She will need 999 coins to buy the lower-level version."
14 October 2020
(update | poll)

Ava's accumulator is no longer destroyed upon a non-PvP death.

9 April 2015
(update)

You can now choose to buy-X devices at once when right-clicking Ava and selecting 'Devices'.

22 January 2015
(update | poll)

The device is now able to grab ammo whether or not an obstacle is in the way.

5 June 2014
(update)

Ava device ammo conservation no longer takes effect on F2P Worlds.

20 February 2014
(update | poll)

Players may now toggle the collection of random metal items by right-clicking the accumulator and selecting "Commune".

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • If your inventory is full when the accumulator picks up a random metal item, it will instead be dropped to the floor. The exception to this rule is if the item that is picked up can be equipped into an empty equipment slot.
