The keris is a dagger obtained after defeating the giant scarab at the end of the Contact! quest, and is the precursor to the keris partisan. It deals 33% bonus damage against all kalphites and scabarites, and has a 1/51 chance of puncturing a hole in their exoskeleton, dealing triple damage. When the special effect occurs, the chatbox will display the game message: You slip your dagger through a gap in the creature's chitin, landing a vicious blow. This message is not displayed if the effect deals less than 10 damage, which can cause the activation frequency to appear lower than it is in reality.[1]

The keris requires 50 Attack to wield. Like most daggers, it can be poisoned. Its stab attack is identical to that of a dragon dagger, although its strength bonus is 10 lower. It has the second-highest bonus of the three prayer bonus-providing daggers, giving +2 compared to the white dagger's +1 and Wolfbane's +5. Despite this, the keris proves superior; unless fighting werewolves, where Wolfbane wins. It is needed to kill a locust rider for a Hard Desert Diary task.

It is lost on death if it is not protected, and can be retrieved from Osman, (or Selim, after Beneath Cursed Sands), for a fee of 20,000. Alternatively, it can be reclaimed from Perdu for a fee of 25,000.

The word "chink" has been swapped with "gap" when players deal a critical hit to kalphites and scabarites with the keris. ("You slip your weapon through a chink in the creature's chitin, landing a vicious blow.")

(update)

The position of the weapon and its variants on female players has been adjusted so that they appear to be equipped properly.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Keris is one of five daggers with its blade pointing down; the other four are the dragon dagger, abyssal dagger, killer's knife, and hunting knife.
  • The keris is based on a real-world weapon, the kris (sometimes krs), originating from the Javanese culture in Indonesia.
