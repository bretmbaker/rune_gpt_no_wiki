The Soulreaper axe is a two-handed axe that requires 80 Attack and Strength to wield. It is created by combining four untradeable components, obtained as drops from The Leviathan, The Whisperer, Vardorvis, and Duke Sucellus, alongside 2,000 blood runes and level 75 Magic (which cannot be boosted).

The axe has a passive on-attack effect, which damages the wielder 8 Hitpoints, and grants a "Soul stack" for every attack they make. As long as a player is not at the maximum amount of stacks (5) and has more than 8 hitpoints, they will take damage and receive one stack of this effect. Each Soul stack provides a 6% boost to a player's Strength Level, up to a maximum of +30% at five stacks. These stacks can be used for the Special Attack Behead, or lost at the rate of one per 20 ticks (12 seconds) of the player not attacking. The player is healed for 8 hitpoints for each Soul stack lost over time or otherwise used through the special attack.

In circumstances where all five Soul stacks are consistently applied to the player, this weapon can deal substantially more damage than other single-target two-handed weapons.

Despite being made from items dropped by prominent Zarosians, the item itself does not grant god protection towards Zarosian NPCs in the God Wars Dungeon.

Soul stacks[edit | edit source]

Soul stacks are generated every time a player makes an attack, even if that attack was not successful hit. Each stack is added after the damage from the player's attack is calculated; so an attack does not benefit from the 6% bonus it just generated.

Each Soul stack provides a 6% boost to a player's Strength Level, up to a maximum of +30% at five stacks. This boost is additive with, and works similarly to the Strength multipliers from prayers like Piety or Ultimate Strength. Soul stacks do not affect other weapons. Because of the way this passive is calculated, the Soulreaper axe can deal substantially more damage than similar weapons like the elder maul or Scythe of Vitur in situations where the player can sustain the full +30% bonus; The difference due to this multiplier becomes even greater when the player is under the effect of other boosts like potions or prayers.

Soul stacks are maintained as long as a player is actively attacking with the Soulreaper axe equipped. In areas with aggressive enemies, very little input is required to keep the strength buff maximized, this makes the axe exceptional for constant combat like Slayer Tasks and the Nightmare Zone.

Health management[edit | edit source]

Players using the Soulreaper axe can expect to lose 40 Hitpoints over 15 seconds when starting a fresh fight with zero Soul stacks. The damage each stack deals can be healed or restored immediately and should be carefully taken into account when fighting dangerous enemies. Players do not take any additional damage from attacking if they already have the max of five Soul stacks.

If leaving combat with five Soul stacks a player will be healed 8 Hitpoints every 20 ticks (12 seconds) for a total of 40 Hitpoints (over 60 seconds). Because Soul stacks do not overheal, 8 hitpoints of healing is lost if a player is at full health and a stack is used or expires. In fights where the player cannot guarantee an attack at least once every 12 seconds, the mechanic can be hard to manage and otherwise result in wasted food or healing.

Swapping gear quickly to restore health at the beginning of combat can alleviate food mismanagement, such as with the special attack from the Saradomin godsword, the Ancient godsword or passive leech from an amulet of blood fury.

Special attack[edit | edit source]

The Soulreaper axe has a special attack, Behead, which consumes all Soul Stacks. This attack does not cost special attack energy and can be used as long as the player has at least one Soul stack.

For each Soul Stack consumed, Behead increases the player's accuracy and damage by 6% and heals them by 8 Hitpoints, effectively restoring any health spent to create those Soul stacks. Players are healed regardless of whether the attack was a successful hit or not. If a player is at full health and has active Soul stacks they can utilize them as a temporary health bank; using Behead to restore up to 40 hitpoints instantly when needed.

Although the in-game description states Behead deals additional damage, the special attack's increased accuracy is the only significant benefit. This is because the Strength boost from Soul stacks is consumed first and a separate bonus is applied to the swing at a different stage in the damage calculation. This typically results in Behead dealing similar maximum hits as a player that continues to auto attack with the Soul stack boost active. It's unknown if this is intentional behavior at this time, as this can result in Behead dealing slightly less damage than an auto attack would otherwise in some situations.

The accuracy roll for Behead uses the attack bonus of the player's currently selected style, which is rolled against the corresponding defensive bonuses of an opponent â€“ either slash or crush in this case.[1] This is in contrast to most other special attacks, which roll against the defensive bonuses of an opponent of a specific combat style regardless of the attacking combat style chosen (for example, the dragon dagger's special attack will always roll against the opponent's slash defence, even if the attack style is set to stab).

Equipping the Soulreaper axe replaces the special attack interface with a unique graphic that indicates the Soul stack resource, however player's special attack energy continues to regenerate in the background normally. Since the special attack only consumes Soul stacks, it's useful to combo Behead into special attacks from other weapons. Some notable examples being the dragon warhammer to reduce an enemy's stats, or weapons like the granite maul for high amounts of burst damage.

A bug has been fixed that was causing lost soul stacks to lower the max hit of other weapons.

(update)
  • Soul Stacks no longer disappear upon switching weapons.
  • The duration in which players can stop attacking without losing Soul Stacks has been doubled.
  • Soul Stacks lost from not attacking anything will now heal the player rather than disappearing completely.
10 January 2024
(update)

The soulreaper axe's non-special attack animations no longer stalls the attacker's movement.

(update)

The soulreaper axe now sends a filterable message to alert players that Soul Stacks are lost when the axe is unequipped.

(update)
  • The soulreaper axe will no longer prevent a player from using its special attack if they do not have enough regular special attack energy.
  • If a Phoenix necklace's healing effect procs, it will no longer reset Soul Stacks.
  • Players can no longer pull up an interface to stall the Soul Stack timer.

An issue with the special attack that caused it to deal extremely high amounts of damage (up to 529) has been fixed.
