Sherlock is the Treasure Trails master, who players must speak to if their elite or master clue directs them to do so. He can be found wandering the woodland between the Sorcerer's Tower and Keep Le Faye.

Getting there[edit | edit source]

  • Using the teleport option of the Kandarin headgear 3 or Kandarin headgear 4.
  • Using the Quest Cape to teleport outside of the Legends' Guild then heading north above the guild.
  • Fairy ring code BLR to teleport south-east of the Legends' Guild then heading north above the guild.
  • Skills necklace teleport option to the Fishing Guild and heading east
  • Combat bracelet teleport option to the Ranging Guild and heading east
  • Camelot teleport to Seers' village and head south
  • Ardougne Cloak 2 or higher to the Ardougne farm patch and heading north-east

Description[edit | edit source]

Sherlock will assign players a skill challenge, which may consist of one of the following (temporary skill boosts may be used where applicable). Once complete, he will give players their next clue or reward.

Note that for the skill challenge to count as completed, the challenge scroll must be held in the inventory when the action is performed. If the challenge is completed but the challenge scroll is not in the player's inventory, it will not be counted and the player must do it again with the challenge scroll in their inventory.

Changes[edit | edit source]

Date Changes
31 March 2016
(update)

Sherlock no longer requires you to use a clue scroll on him in order to get a challenge scroll. You can now simply talk to Sherlock with your clue scroll in your inventory.

17 September 2015
(update)

The 'Wedge' now tells you the correct amount of teleports to Sherlock for completing the Elite Kandarin diary in his reward dialogue.

23 July 2015
(update)

Sherlock's scarf will no longer be visible through the back of the NPC and his waist line will no longer be transparent.

12 March 2015
(update)

Crafting anything with leather no longer completes an elite skill challenge.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The character is a reference to Arthur Conan Doyle's character Sherlock Holmes.
  • When your character observes that he has no shirt on, he will reply with "No shirt, Sherlock". This is a play on the phrase "no shit, Sherlock", an insult used when somebody points out an obvious fact.
  • When using an item on Sherlock, he'll say: "That's nice, but... I don't really need one of those."
