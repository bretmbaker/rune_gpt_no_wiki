Yewnock the Engineer is the son of Oaknock the Engineer, the legendary gnome builder. Like his father, Yewnock built a machine that would dispel illusions, as seen in a cutscene during The Path of Glouphrie where he exposes Bolrie's advisor as Glouphrie the Untrusted.

His workshop and machine are located in the Gnome Village Dungeon, where the player uses it to expose King Bolren's pet as an evil creature.

He is known for developing gnome gliders, which were based on the toys his father used to make.[1] Nothing else is known about him or his sibling(s), but given the lifespan of gnomes and the fact that the test pilots of his first prototypes are still alive, it is possible that Yewnock still lives.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Yewnock the Engineer's examine text is a reference to the British animated children's TV series Bob the Builder, with Bob's catchphrase being "Can he fix it? Yes he can!"
  • While the name of his father Oaknock is derived from Oak trees, Yewnock shares a similar trait in his name but with Yew trees. Both of these trees exist as woodcutting trees in the game.
  • The gnome glider display at the top floor of Varrock Museum misspells Yewnock's name as "Yewknock" in the display's description.
