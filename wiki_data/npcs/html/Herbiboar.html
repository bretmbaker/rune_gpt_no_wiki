The herbiboar is a creature that players can capture with 80 Hunter and 31 Herblore, granting between 1,950 and 2,461 Hunter experience. It can be caught by first tracking down footsteps left across Fossil Island, until reaching a point where the footsteps enter a burrow. Players can then kick the burrow, causing the herbiboar to appear and get stunned just outside the hole. Players may then harvest between one and three herbs off the herbiboar's back before it flees. Secateurs are not required to harvest the herbiboar, but having magic secateurs equipped will allow players to harvest an additional herb. This effect does apply when the magic secateurs are in the player's inventory.

Herbiboars may be assigned as a hunters' rumours task by Guild Hunters Gilman, Teco, or Wolf, but only after Bone Voyage has been completed.

Hunting technique[edit | edit source]

To begin tracking a herbiboar, inspect the rock, mushroom, or log next to the hunter icon () on the minimap. There are a total of 5 spots where you can start. Continue by inspecting seaweed, smelly and normal mushrooms, and muddy patches around the area to find more tracks until a tunnel containing a herbiboar is revealed; attack this tunnel and harvest the appearing herbiboar. Additionally, the player has a chance to fail at tracking down the creature. When this occurs, a message will appear in the chatbox, stating: The creature has successfully confused you with its tracks, leading you round in circles. You'll need to start again.

Warning: The herbiboar will disappear if a new track is started before collecting its herbs.

Notes[edit | edit source]

  • Since only the beginning step of tracking a herbiboar requires level 80 Hunter, hunter potions and spicy stews can be used to temporarily boost from level 77 or level 75, respectively; however, doing so will grant less experience, as it scales on the base Hunter level of the player.
  • The ring of pursuit's effect cannot be used to track herbiboars.
  • It is advised to bring a herb sack to reduce the amount of banking. Alternatively, the tool leprechaun next to the hardwood tree patches can be used to note them.
  • The graceful outfit is valuable while hunting herbiboars, due to its stamina regenerating properties while inspecting objects while tracking the herbiboar.
  • Stamina potions will significantly increase the amount of herbiboars that can be caught per hour, as a vast majority of the time spent tracking will be running.
  • At level 66 Magic the Arceuus spell vile vigour can be used to convert prayer points into run energy if you'd rather spend soul runes instead of stamina potions. This method gets better as your prayer level increases, and is not recommended unless you have an altar (or pool) and digsite pendant in your house to recharge your prayer since there are no altars on the island.
  • The ring of endurance is very valuable for hunting herbiboars, as it will effectively halve the inventory slots used for stamina potions and reduce run energy drain.

Guaranteed tracks[edit | edit source]

There are a few instances in which the player will always find the next part of the track by inspecting the same object every time.

  • A - When this spot is approached from the south, the path will always continue at the smelly mushroom.
  • C - When this spot is approached from the west, the path will always continue at the muddy patch.
  • D - When this spot is approached from the west, the path will always continue at the muddy patch.
  • E - When this spot is approached from the north, the path will always continue at the smelly mushroom.
  • F - When this spot is approached from the east, the path will always continue at the smelly mushroom.
  • G - When this spot is approached from the north, the path will always continue at the smelly mushroom.
  • H - When this spot is approached from the north, the path will always continue at the north-eastern seaweed.
  • H - When this spot is approached from the east, the path will always continue at the south-western seaweed.
  • I - When this spot is approached from the east, the path will always continue at the muddy patch, if this was the first part of the track.
  • I - When this spot is approached from the south, the path will always continue at the muddy patch.
  • I - When this spot is approached from the west, the path will always continue at the smelly mushroom.

Experience and harvesting[edit | edit source]

An inspection of the herbiboar's trail can grant 50 experience and occasionally gives numulite or unidentified fossils. Once the player attacks the tunnel at the end of a trail, a herbiboar appears and Hunter experience is gained depending on the player's Hunter level. After the herbiboar appears, it can be harvested, requiring at least level 31 Herblore.

Experience gained from attacking the tunnel at the end of a trail scales depending on the base Hunter level (without considering boosts) of the player. The base value granted is 1,950 experience at level 80 Hunter, with 30 additional experience gained per Hunter level above 80 until level 94. Strangely, 15 additional experience is granted per Hunter level at level 95, instead of the normal 30 additional experience. Afterwards, 19 additional experience is gained per level from 95 to 99 Hunter.[1]

Harvesting from the herbiboar earns the player experience, herbs, and a chance to obtain the pet Herbi. The amount of herbs picked is a random number 1–3, and can be increased to 2–4 if magic secateurs are equipped or held in the player's inventory. For each herb picked except for the first, 25 Herblore experience is granted.

An average of 59–66 herbiboars can be caught per hour while using stamina potions. This makes the experience rates at level 80 somewhere in between 115,000–128,000, only increasing as Hunter level increases. A considerably lower number will be caught per hour if not using stamina potions, due to the amount of running required. A weight of 0 kg or lower is highly recommended, as well as the herb sack. Since fossils weigh a bit, it is recommended to store them occasionally in the storage box by the mycelium pool.

Harvesting the herbiboar[edit | edit source]

The quality of herbs dropped by the herbiboar is determined by the player's Herblore level.[2] Some low-level herbs disappear from the drop table entirely at a certain Herblore level, while other high-level herbs require a certain Herblore level threshold to be included in the drop table. See the table below for ranges [3] Toadflax and Huasca are the only herbs that are not dropped at all. The probability of receiving ranarr weeds or other high-tier herbs scales with the player's Herblore level.[4] Quantity of herbs dropped does not scale with levels. The Farming cape does not yield any benefits for harvesting herbiboar.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The harvesting animation will show the player holding secateurs even if none are present in the inventory.
  • The herbiboar greatly resembles the bushpig, a pig species native to South Africa.
  • The name "herbiboar" is a portmanteau of "herbivore" and "boar".
  • The herbiboar's examine text is a reference to the popular adage "A rose by any other name would smell as sweet", from William Shakespeare's play Romeo and Juliet.
  • The first sound effect that gets played when attacking the final tunnel the herbiboar resides in is the same sound effect as firing a longbow (id: 2700).
