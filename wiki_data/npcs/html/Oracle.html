The Oracle is a strange woman who lives atop Ice Mountain. She is an important figure in the Dragon Slayer I quest, as she provides a clue as to how to access one of the map pieces. Occasionally, she will be visited by Postie Pete. One of the Wise Old Man's tasks involves taking a message to her. To reach Oracle, use the Ice Mountain pathway just below Edgeville Monastery.

As with Uri, many of her quotes are jokes or references to things in and outside of the game.

Original Purpose[edit | edit source]

According to a posting by Andrew in June 1999 (one of the first references to his RPG):

“ "I'm (very slowly) writing my own Java RPG, and eventually I want to have this oracle type thing up a mountain where you can go, and it will give you a hint on your current quest. Obviously you should make it fairly hard to get to. (i.e put it out of the way, and protect it with some monsters) but it does stop people getting completely and utterly stuck!" ”
 
— Andrew Gower [1]

Trivia[edit | edit source]

General[edit | edit source]

  • In ancient times, oracles would live on top of mountains, and were consulted by the kings and leaders of countries. They were considered close to the gods (as the Oracle in RuneScape is as well).

Phrases[edit | edit source]

Game-related[edit | edit source]

  • "They say that ham does not mix well with other kinds of meat", is a pun and reference to the human-supremacist organisation H.A.M., of which the members do not "mix well" with the other races of Gielinor.
  • "Do not fear the dragons...fear their kin", is a reference to the Dragonkin.
  • "Is it time to wake up? I am not sure...", is very likely a reference to the prophesied event of Guthix waking from his slumber.
  • Several phrases refer to the Stone of Jas:
    • " Jas left a stone behind."
    • "The God Wars are over...as long as the thing they were fighting over remains hidden."
    • "The great snake of Guthix guards more than she knows."

Other[edit | edit source]

  • "A woodchuck does not chuck wood", is a reference to the tongue twister "How much wood would a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood?"
  • "Who guards the guardsmen?", is a reference to Quis custodiet ipsos custodes?, a Latin phrase meaning "Who will guard the guards themselves?", or alternatively "Who watches the watchmen?", generally attributed to the Roman poet Juvenal.
  • "When in Asgarnia, do like Asgarnians do", is a reference to the phrase "When in Rome, do as Romans do".
  • "Don't judge a book by its cover - judge it on its' grammar and, punctuation.", is a joke based on the fact that the sentence contains incorrect grammar throughout.
