The white rabbit is a rabbit that players can capture with the Hunter skill at level 27 or above, granting 144 Hunter experience. Completion of the Eagles' Peak quest is also required.

Hunting spot location[edit | edit source]

Location of the white rabbits in the Piscatoris Hunter area.

Hunting technique[edit | edit source]

The hunting technique is unique for this creature. First off, it requires a ferret, which can be caught in the same area by using a box trap. Additionally, a couple of rabbit snares are needed.

Upon flushing a rabbit hole, with a ferret in the inventory, a rabbit will leave a random hole and flight into one of the other holes. In order to catch the rabbit, the player has to set up rabbit snares along the paths between the holes, so that the rabbit will run into the snare and get caught.

Multiple traps[edit | edit source]

Rabbit snaring can be done with multiple traps at once, with the maximum amount of traps depending on the player's Hunter level.

Uses[edit | edit source]

A rabbit foot can be made into a strung rabbit foot at level 37 Crafting by using a ball of wool on it. A strung rabbit foot will increase the chance of a bird nest containing a bird's egg.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Despite being called 'white rabbits', their fur appears to be light brown or beige.
  • The white rabbit cannot be examined, unlike virtually all other creatures in RuneScape.
