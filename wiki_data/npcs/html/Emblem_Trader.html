The Emblem Trader is a mysterious merchant who runs the Bounty Hunter Store. His store sells various items for use in the Bounty Hunter minigame, such as Ancient Warriors' equipment and weapon imbue scrolls. On Deadman Mode worlds he runs the Bounty Hunter Shop (Deadman Mode) instead.

He can skull the player should they ask him to, and can change the player's Target matchmaking range to be either 5, 10, or 15 combat levels within their own in Bounty Hunter.

He also wanders the Revenant Caves in search of ancient artefacts. Players can give him ancient artefacts (either noted or un-noted) to receive a large amount of coins. However, he will only accept them on the official Bounty Hunter world. This trader cannot open the Bounty Hunter store, as he states that it is too dangerous to do so.

As an alternative to taking coins into the Bounty Hunter Minigame, which is necessary to get a Target, players may use the Emblem Trader's coffer to deposit the required amount of coins up to a maximum of 2,000,000 coins.

Historical stock[edit | edit source]

Mysterious emblems[edit | edit source]

Players could purchase mysterious emblems from the Emblem Trader; there were five tiers of emblem with increasing values. Once a mysterious emblem was purchased, players could upgrade the emblem or sell it back to the Emblem Trader for the same amount of coins. Players could not buy a tier 5 emblem; they were obtainable only by upgrading a tier 4 emblem.

The price of a mysterious emblem depended on its tier:

Emblem Buy price Sell price
Tier 1 75,000 75,000
Tier 2 150,000 150,000
Tier 3 300,000 300,000
Tier 4 600,000 600,000
Tier 5 N/A 1,200,000

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • The Emblem Trader is supposedly allied with one of the Mahjarrat, as when you give him a mysterious emblem he will say "Thank you. My master to the North will be pleased."
  • He appears to be wearing olive-green coloured elder chaos druid robes and a enchanted slayer staff.
